# Tanan & Purbojo (2025) – Pengaruh Persepsi Guru Tentang Budaya Sekolah dan Gaya Kepemimpinan Digital terhadap Perilaku Kerja Inovatif Guru di Sekolah XYZ Manado

**Summary**: This study examines the influence of school culture and digital leadership on the innovative work behavior (IWB) of teachers in a private school in Manado, finding that while school culture significantly predicts IWB, digital leadership does not in this specific context.

**Sources**: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Dalpin Tanan (Universitas Pelita Harapan), Rijanto Purbojo (Universitas Pelita Harapan)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Kelola: Jurnal Manajemen Pendidikan, 12(1), 31-46
- **Methodology**: Quantitative (Correlation)
- **Sample**: 63 teachers, Private School (XYZ Manado), Indonesia
- **Analysis**: PLS-SEM (SmartPLS)
- **Data collection**: Questionnaires (Likert scale 1-4), September 2023 – May 2024

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[school-culture]] (measured by indicators like collaboration, professional development, and collaborative planning)
- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (measured by digital skills and implementation of digital systems)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (measured by exploration, generation, promotion, implementation, and sustainability of ideas)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Budaya Sekolah → IWB (+) | **Supported** (P-value = 0.000) |
| H2 | Kepemimpinan Digital → IWB (+) | **Not supported** (P-value = 0.445) |

## Key Findings

### School Culture and IWB
- School culture has a positive and significant impact on teacher IWB (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 40).
- Teachers show a positive tendency towards IWB when they receive opportunities for collaboration, feedback on learning plans, and professional development programs (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 40).
- The presence of optimistic leaders and sustainable mentoring in the teaching-learning process supports teacher innovation (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 40).

### Digital Leadership and IWB
- Digital leadership does not have a significant influence on IWB in this context (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 41).
- The non-significance is attributed to teachers already being independent in technology use (e.g., MS Teams, PMM) and a leadership focus that prioritized curriculum/learning over just technology (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 41).
- This finding contrasts with earlier studies in IT sectors, suggesting that digital leadership in education has different priority areas (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 41).

## Contradictions
- **Digital Leadership**: Conflicts with findings in IT sectors (e.g., Sagbas et al., 2023) where digital leadership significantly predicts innovative behavior. This study suggests context-dependent efficacy of digital leadership (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 41).

## Practical Implications
- Schools should focus on building a collaborative culture and providing constructive feedback to foster teacher innovation.
- Digital leadership in education should be integrated with school management aspects such as quality, satisfaction, and digital supervision, rather than just technical implementation.

## Limitation
- **Stated**: The study was limited to a specific private school context (XYZ Manado).
- **Critical Analysis**: The sample size (N=63) is relatively small for PLS-SEM, which may affect the generalizability of the findings. The lack of a neutral option in the 4-point Likert scale might force respondents to take a side, potentially skewing data.
