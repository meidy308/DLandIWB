# Nugroho et al. (2025) – The effect of digital leadership on innovative work behavior: The mediating role of cultural moral and work engagement

**Summary**: This study investigates how digital leadership influences innovative work behavior through the mediating roles of organizational culture (referred to as "cultural moral" in some sections) and work engagement in an Indonesian state-owned construction company.

**Sources**: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Heru Nugroho, Adi Saputro, Dadet Sugiharto (Universitas Budi Luhur, Jakarta)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Jurnal Fokus Manajemen Bisnis, 15(1), 84-101
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 205 employees of Construction Company X (State-Owned Enterprise), Indonesia
- **Analysis**: SEM-PLS (SmartPLS)
- **Data collection**: Questionnaire, 205 respondents randomly selected from 2,269 employees

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (Erhan et al., 2022)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Erhan et al., 2022)
- **Mediator 1**: [[organizational-culture]] (Robbins and Judge, 2018)
- **Mediator 2**: [[work-engagement]] (Meynaar et al., 2021)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | DL → IWB (+) | **Not supported** (B = 0.010, p = 0.853) |
| H2 | DL → OC (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.311, p = 0.000) |
| H3 | DL → WE (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.315, p = 0.001) |
| H4 | OC → IWB (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.488, p = 0.000) |
| H5 | WE → IWB (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.417, p = 0.000) |
| H6 | DL → OC → IWB | **Supported** (B = 0.153, p = 0.000) |
| H7 | DL → WE → IWB | **Supported** (B = 0.132, p = 0.000) |

## Key Findings

### Non-significant Direct Effect of Digital Leadership
Digital leadership does not directly have a significant effect on innovative work behavior in the construction sector context (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 84, 92). This suggests that leaders in this environment act as triggers who must first shape the organizational environment and employee mindset before innovation can occur.

### Full Mediation through Culture and Engagement
The relationship between digital leadership and IWB is fully mediated by organizational culture and work engagement.
- Digital leadership positively influences organizational culture by persuading others to face change and achieve sustainable goals (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 87, 92).
- Digital leadership fosters high work engagement by providing direction and motivation (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 88, 92).

### Contextual Relevance (Construction Sector)
The construction sector needs to follow technological innovation to stay competitive. Digital leadership provides direction for organizational change and motivation for subordinates to have a high level of engagement to innovate in their work (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 84-85).

## Practical Implications

- Construction company management can increase IWB by focusing on the implementation of digital leadership to form a "good" organizational culture and strong work engagement (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 95).
- Leaders should educate employees to understand the organizational culture which is useful for sustainability and shaping employee behavior (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 88).

## Limitation

- The study does not differentiate respondents based on their level of position (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 95).
- Conducted only at one state-owned construction company; results might differ in private companies (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 95).
- (Critical Analysis): The discussion section in the original source is partially garbled with unrelated content about "Impulse Buying," which may indicate issues in the final editing or formatting of the source paper.
