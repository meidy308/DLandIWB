# Erhan et al. (2022) – From Conventional to Digital Leadership: Exploring Digitalization of Leadership and Innovative Work Behavior

**Summary**: A study demonstrating that the employees' perception of digital leadership has a positive and significant effect on all four dimensions of innovative work behavior in the Turkish textile industry.

**Sources**: Erhan et al. - 2022.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Tugba Erhan & Hasan Huseyin Uzunbacak (Suleyman Demirel University), Erhan Aydin (Usak University / IPAG Business School Paris)
- **Year**: 2022
- **Journal**: Management Research Review, 45(11), 1524–1543
- **Methodology**: Quantitative, cross-sectional survey
- **Sample**: 320 department managers in the textile industry in Denizli, Turkey (50.3% women, 78.8% married, 83.1% bachelor's degree, mean age 38.5, mean tenure 14.6 years)
- **Analysis**: SEM path analysis via AMOS 24, Bootstrap 5000 samples, 95% bias-corrected CI; SPSS 26 for descriptive statistics
- **Data collection**: Questionnaires distributed Sep–Oct 2021 to 440 people across 95 textile companies (76% response from contacted firms); 320 usable responses

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (6-item "Informatics Leadership Scale" — orientation dimension only; 5-point Likert; Ulutaş & Arslan, 2017)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] — 4 dimensions:
  - Idea exploration (IE, 2 items)
  - Idea generation (IG, 3 items)
  - Idea championing (IC, 2 items)
  - Idea implementation (II, 3 items)
  - (10 items total, 5-point Likert; De Jong & Den Hartog, 2010; Turkish adaptation Çimen & Yücel, 2017)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | DL → Idea Exploration (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.64, β = 0.56, C.R. = 11.978, p < 0.001, CI [0.462, 0.638]) |
| H2 | DL → Idea Generation (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.54, β = 0.60, C.R. = 13.369, p < 0.001, CI [0.514, 0.670]) |
| H3 | DL → Idea Championing (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.86, β = 0.70, C.R. = 17.353, p < 0.001, CI [0.620, 0.721]) |
| H4 | DL → Idea Implementation (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.48, β = 0.48, C.R. = 9.705, p < 0.001, CI [0.357, 0.577]) |

## Key Findings

### Digital leadership predicts all four IWB dimensions
All four hypotheses were supported. Digital leadership has the **strongest standardized effect on idea championing** (β = 0.70), followed by idea generation (β = 0.60), idea exploration (β = 0.56), and idea implementation (β = 0.48) (source: Erhan et al. - 2022.md, p. 1535–1536).

### Overall DL–IWB correlation
Digital leadership has a strong and significant overall correlation with innovative work behavior (r = 0.787, p < 0.01) (source: Erhan et al. - 2022.md, p. 1535).

### Measurement model fit
Five-factor model showed perfect fit: χ²/df = 2.779, CFI = 0.96, NFI = 0.94, TLI = 0.95, RMSEA = 0.076 (source: Erhan et al. - 2022.md, p. 1534).

### Structural model fit
Good fit indices: χ²/df = 1.31, CFI = 0.99, NFI = 0.99, TLI = 0.99, RMSEA = 0.031 (source: Erhan et al. - 2022.md, p. 1536).

### Theoretical grounding
The study is grounded in the **Upper Echelons Theory** (Hambrick & Mason, 1984), which posits that strong managers significantly affect strategic decisions, and their digital competencies serve as a source of organizational power (source: Erhan et al. - 2022.md, p. 1527).

## Contradictions

- This study finds **strong direct effects** of DL on all IWB dimensions (β = 0.48–0.70), without mediators. In contrast, [[ahmed-et-al-2024]] found no significant direct DL → IWB path — the entire effect was sequentially mediated through learning orientation and innovation capabilities.
- The overall DL–IWB correlation (r = 0.787) is considerably higher than what was found in other studies (e.g., r = 0.348 in Çetinkaya & Sürücü, 2025). This may reflect sector-specific factors (the textile industry in Denizli is a pilot digitalization area) or the use of a different DL scale (Informatics Leadership vs. Zeike et al.).

## Practical Implications

- Digitally skilled leaders stimulate innovative approaches of employees, leading to higher motivation and performance. Organizations should develop digital leadership competencies beyond traditional management skills (source: Erhan et al. - 2022.md, p. 1537).
- Organizations in developing countries like Turkey need to follow digitalization closely and implement it rapidly to compete globally. The COVID-19 pandemic underscored the advantage of organizations managed by digitally competent leaders (source: Erhan et al. - 2022.md, p. 1537).
- The textile sector — as a pilot digitalization area — demonstrates that even traditional manufacturing industries benefit significantly from digital leadership in fostering innovation (source: Erhan et al. - 2022.md, p. 1526, 1537).
