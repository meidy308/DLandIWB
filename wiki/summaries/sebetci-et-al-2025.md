# Sebetci et al. (2025) – Examining the Impact of Digital Leadership on Innovative Work Behavior and Knowledge Sharing Behavior Through Psychological Ownership

**Summary**: This study investigates how digital leadership influences employee innovation and knowledge sharing through the mediating mechanism of psychological ownership (emotional and work-based) in the IT sector.

**Sources**: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Özel Sebetci (Adnan Menderes University), Muhammet Nimet Çavuş (Adnan Menderes University), Baran Çavuş (Ankara University)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: International Journal of Sociotechnology and Knowledge Development, 17(1), 1-15
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 287 managers and employees in IT departments within technology parks, Ankara, Turkey
- **Analysis**: PLS-SEM (SmartPLS 4.0), Variance Accounted For (VAF) for mediation
- **Data collection**: Online survey, February 23 – April 3, 2023

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (Supportive and Innovative dimensions; scale by Büyükbeşe et al., 2022)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (De Jong & Den Hartog, 2008), [[knowledge-sharing-behavior]] (Usefulness/Appropriateness dimension; Alakurt & Keser, 2016)
- **Mediator**: [[psychological-ownership]] (Emotional and Work-Based ownership; Shukla & Singh, 2015)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Digital Leadership → Psychological Ownership → Innovative Work Behavior | **Supported** (Partial to Full mediation) |
| H1.1 | Innovative DL → Emotional PO → IWB | **Supported** (VAF = 50.07%, z = 7.006, p < .001) |
| H1.2 | Innovative DL → Work-Based PO → IWB | **Supported** (VAF = 77.18%, z = 10.95, p < .001) |
| H1.3 | Supportive DL → Emotional PO → IWB | **Supported** (VAF = 56.48%, z = 7.68, p < .001) |
| H1.4 | Supportive DL → Work-Based PO → IWB | **Supported (Full Mediation)** (VAF = 80.91%, z = 9.92, p < .001) |
| H2 | Digital Leadership → Psychological Ownership → Knowledge Sharing Behavior | **Supported** (Partial mediation) |
| H2.1 | Innovative DL → Emotional PO → KSB | **Supported** (VAF = 55.08%, z = 7.04, p < .001) |
| H2.2 | Innovative DL → Work-Based PO → KSB | **Supported** (VAF = 78.77%, z = 8.93, p < .001) |
| H2.3 | Supportive DL → Emotional PO → KSB | **Supported** (VAF = 56.46%, z = 7.43, p < .001) |
| H2.4 | Supportive DL → Work-Based PO → KSB | **Supported** (VAF = 76.00%, z = 8.60, p < .001) |

## Key Findings

- **Psychological Ownership as a Core Mechanism**: The study demonstrates that digital leadership's influence on innovation and knowledge sharing is largely psychologically constructed through feelings of responsibility and ownership (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).
- **Task vs. Emotional Attachment**: Work-based ownership emerged as a stronger mediator than emotional ownership, suggesting that tech professionals respond more deeply to task-level alignment than affective connection (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).
- **Transformation via Support**: Supportive digital leadership enhances innovation primarily by reinforcing task-level psychological ownership, rather than through direct action (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).
- **Digital Growth**: Both innovative and supportive dimensions of digital leadership are pivotal for digital transformation success, as they mitigate technostress and align organizational strategy with individual competencies (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 2).

## Contradictions

- None noted relative to previous sources, but explicitly highlights that task-based alignment is more critical than affective connection in tech contexts (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).

## Practical Implications

- Organizations should invest in leadership training that builds both digital competencies and psychological empowerment.
- Leaders should grant greater autonomy and responsibility to foster work-based ownership.
- Management should recognize that IWB and KSB are outcomes of internalization rather than just technological adaptation.

## Limitation

- **Stated**: Cross-sectional design (limits causality); Geographic concentration in Ankara, Turkey; Reliance on self-reported data (common method bias).
- **Critical Analysis**: The use of only the "Usefulness/Appropriateness" dimension of knowledge sharing may miss other critical aspects like tacit knowledge transfer or willingness to share despite competition. The geographical focus on technology parks may skew results toward highly motivated/digitally literate populations compared to traditional SME sectors.
