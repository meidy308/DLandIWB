# Demerouti (2022) – Turn Digitalization and Automation to a Job Resource

**Summary**: This commentary discusses how digitalization and automation can be designed as job resources rather than demands. It outlines five essential conditions for creating "healthy" digital jobs and emphasizes the role of job crafting and proactive organizational design.

## 5 Conditions for Healthy Digital Jobs

According to Demerouti (2022, p. 1209), digitalization and automation contribute to stimulating and "healthy" jobs if:
1. They are designed to **support people's work** (rather than replace them).
2. People are in **control** and can **craft** their use.
3. **Job resources are maximized** and job demands are affordable.
4. The **economic growth is shared** among stakeholders, including employees.
5. **Authorities protect** employees and employment conditions.

## Key Findings

### Technology as a Demand or Resource
Critical to the impact of technology is its design. It can be a **resource** if it supports local decision-making, or a **demand** if it increases cognitive load, vigilance requirements, or work-family conflict (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1206).

### Shifting Job Characteristics
Cross-temporal meta-analyses show that while skill variety, task identity, and autonomy have increased between 1975 and 2010, feedback has not (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1205).

### The Risk of Digital Taylorism
Demerouti warns against "left-over" design principles where technology takes over stimulating tasks, leaving humans with repetitive, vigilance-based roles, termed **"Digital Taylorism"** (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1208).

### Flexibility: Employer-led vs. Employee-led
Flexibility in working time/location is a dual-edged sword. **Employee-led flexibility** (decision on start/end time) is a job resource, whereas **employer-led flexibility** (time restrictions, overtime) is a job demand (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1207).

### Proactive Job Crafting
Employees who are stimulated to craft their jobs are more open and adaptive to changes. They can create an environment where they operate optimally and integrate technology into their daily work (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1208).

## Practical Implications

- **System Redesign**: Organizations must redesign whole systems (tasks, knowledge, structure) taking user experiences into account.
- **Protection Norms**: Authorities should introduce norms for safe working hours, income protection, and privacy.
- **Meta-Skills Education**: Institutions should shift from teaching just knowledge to promoting meta-skills like cooperation, creativity, and problem-solving (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1208).

## Limitation

- **Theoretical Scope**: As a commentary, it provides a high-level vision but lacks new empirical data to test the proposed five conditions directly.
- **Short-term vs. Long-term**: Notes that job demands may increase in the short term during implementation but may reduce as employees become acquainted with technology (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1206).
