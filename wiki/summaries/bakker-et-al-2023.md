# Bakker et al. (2023) – Job Demands–Resources Theory: Ten Years Later

**Summary**: A comprehensive review of the past decade of JD-R research, introducing four major innovations: the person × situation approach, multilevel JD-R theory, new proactive behaviors (vitality management and playful design), and the work–home resources model.

**Sources**: Bakker et al. - 2023.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Arnold B. Bakker (Erasmus University), Evangelia Demerouti (Eindhoven University of Technology), and Ana Sanz-Vergel (University of East Anglia)
- **Year**: 2023
- **Journal**: Annual Review of Organizational Psychology and Organizational Behavior, 10, 25-53
- **Methodology**: Literature Review and Theoretical Synthesis
- **Sample**: Synthesis of hundreds of empirical studies, including meta-analyses and 191 new diary studies since 2014.
- **Analysis**: Thematic synthesis and theoretical formalization of 9 propositions.

## Key Findings

### Innovations in JD-R Theory
The authors highlight four major innovations of the past decade:
1. **[[person-situation-approach-jd-r]]**: Personality (Big Five) moderates the daily effects of job characteristics on well-being (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 37).
2. **[[multilevel-jd-r-theory]]**: Integration of organizational (HR practices, climate), leader, and team-level JD-R with individual outcomes (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 38).
3. **New Proactive Approaches**: Introduction of [[proactive-vitality-management]] (changing the self) and [[playful-work-design]] (creating fun/competition) (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 39-40).
4. **[[work-home-resources-model]]**: Spillover and crossover effects between work and home domains through volatile personal resources (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 41).

### The Concept of Burnout and Engagement
- **Burnout**: Now recognized by WHO in ICD-11 as an "occupational phenomenon" (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 26). Meta-analytic overlap with depression is high (*r* = 0.80).
- **Work Engagement**: Characterized by vigor, dedication, and absorption. Prevalence in Europe is high (~71-76%) (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 27).

### Self-Undermining and Loss Cycles
New evidence shows that strain leads to [[self-undermining]] (poor communication, mistakes), which creates a loss cycle by generating new job demands (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 36).

## Contradictions
- **Job Demands and Performance**: Some studies show challenge demands can increase performance, while others focus on the health-impairment path (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 41).
- **Burnout vs. Depression**: High correlation (*r* = 0.80) makes differentiation difficult, yet they are treated as distinct in most JD-R research (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 26).

## Practical Implications
1. **Organizational Level**: Improve psychosocial safety climate and HR practices (learning, development) (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 42).
2. **Leader Level**: Leaders should facilitate resource exchange and model proactive behaviors (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 43).
3. **Individual Level**: Job crafting training, recovery training, and interventions for playful work design and vitality management (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 43).

## Limitation
- **Subjectivity**: Much research still relies on self-reports; more observer-based and objective measures (e.g., financial data, physiological markers) are needed (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 44).
- **Causality**: While longitudinal studies exist, true experimental designs for interventions are still relatively scarce (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 45).
