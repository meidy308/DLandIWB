# Rahmandika et al. (2025) – Empowering Innovation: The Role of Digital Leadership in Shaping Gen Z and Gen Y Performance with Creativity as a Moderator

**Summary**: This study investigates how digital leadership influences employee performance through the mediating role of innovative work behavior, while examining creativity as a boundary condition (moderator) for Gen Z and Gen Y employees.

**Sources**: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Harris Prasetya Rahmandika (BINUS), Yusuf Akbar Santoso (UNAIR), Dilla Imani (UNAIR), Nuri Herachwati (UNAIR)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Jurnal Manajemen Teori dan Terapan (Journal of Theoretical and Applied Management), 18(2), 241-257
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 241 permanent employees (Gen Y: 51.5%, Gen Z: 48.5%) across various industries in Indonesia (predominantly services, 31.1%).
- **Analysis**: PLS-SEM (SmartPLS 4)
- **Data collection**: Online questionnaire via social media.

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (adapted from Zhu, 2022; 6 items focusing on expert knowledge and driving transformation).
- **Dependent variable**: [[employee-performance]] (adapted from de Azevedo Andrade, 2020; 10 items covering task and contextual performance).
- **Mediator**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (adapted from De Jong & Den Hartog, 2010; 10 items covering idea generation, exploration, championing, and implementation).
- **Moderator**: [[problem-solving-creativity]] (adapted from Baer & Oldham, 2006; 4 items).

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.325, p = 0.001) |
| H2 | [[innovative-work-behavior]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.272, p = 0.000) |
| H3 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.264, p = 0.001) |
| H4 | DL → IWB → EP (Mediation) | **Supported** (β = 0.088, p = 0.018 - Partial Mediation) |
| H5 | Creativity moderates DL → EP | **Supported (Negative)** (β = -0.134, p = 0.007) |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Negative Moderation of Creativity**: Contrary to expectations of a "strengthening" effect, high creativity actually **weakened** the impact of digital leadership on performance. This suggests that highly creative Gen Z/Y employees value autonomy and may perceive direct digital leadership as unnecessary or redundant (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 251).

## Key Findings

### Digital Leadership as Performance Catalyst
Digital leadership significantly enhances both innovation and performance. Leaders who are perceived as digital experts and proactive drivers of transformation create an environment where young employees feel motivated to excel (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 250).

### The Mediating Role of Innovation
IWB acting as a partial mediator confirms that digital leadership's impact on performance is not just direct, but also functions by stimulating behaviors like idea generation and implementation (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 250).

### The Creativity Paradox for Gen Z & Y
The study reveals a unique generational nuance: when creativity is already high, the relative importance of digital leadership for performance decreases. Highly creative individuals in the "digital native" generations (Gen Y/Z) often seek independence and internalize digital standards, making them less reliant on leader direction (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 251).

## Practical Implications

- **For Leaders**: Managers should shift from being "directors" to "facilitators" of innovation, especially for highly creative talent.
- **For Organizations**: Use OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) to provide boundaries for creative output without stifling autonomy (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 252).
- **Digital Infrastructure**: Implement collaboration tools (Slack, Teams, Trello) to support the "idea championing" and "implementation" phases of IWB (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 252).

## Limitation

- **Methodological**: Reliance on self-reported data may lead to common method bias (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 253).
- **Critical Analysis**: The study does not account for digital maturity of the organization or infrastructure readiness, which might influence how "expert" a leader needs to be.
