# Gehan & Yulianti (2024) – Pengaruh Digital Leadership Terhadap Perilaku Kerja Inovatif dan Dampaknya Pada Kinerja Karyawan Milenial di Jawa Timur Dengan Dimoderasi oleh Leader Member Exchange (LMX)

**Summary**: This study investigates how digital leadership influences innovative work behavior and employee performance among millennials, finding that leadership's impact on performance is fully mediated by innovation.

**Sources**: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Alifito Zhafir Gehan & Emma Yulianti (Universitas Hayam Wuruk Perbanas)
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: Jurnal Manajemen STIE Muhammadiyah Palopo, 10(1), 29-40
- **Methodology**: Quantitative, Descriptive
- **Sample**: 105 millennial employees (aged 27-42) in East Java, Indonesia
- **Analysis**: SEM-PLS (SmartPLS 4.0)
- **Data collection**: Online questionnaire (Google Form)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (Attitude, Competence, Behavior, Skill)
- **Dependent variable**: [[employee-performance]] (Task, Contextual, Adaptive Performance)
- **Mediator**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Idea generation, promotion, and realization)
- **Moderator**: [[leader-member-exchange]] (Affect, Loyalty, Contribution, Professional Respect)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Not supported** (T = 0.732, p = 0.464) |
| H2 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Supported** (O = 0.606, T = 6.263, p = 0.000) |
| H3 | [[innovative-work-behavior]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Supported** (O = 0.541, T = 4.432, p = 0.000) |
| H4 | [[leader-member-exchange]] moderates [[innovative-work-behavior]] → [[employee-performance]] | **Not supported** (T = 0.510, p = 0.610) |

## Key Findings

### The Primacy of Innovation as a Mediator
Digital leadership does not directly improve performance for millennials in East Java. Instead, its value lies in fostering [[innovative-work-behavior]] ($O = 0.606, p = .000$), which then serves as the primary engine for enhancing performance ($O = 0.541, p = .000$) (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 36-37).

### Non-Significant Moderation of LMX
Although high-quality relationships (LMX) are often thought to amplify behavioral outcomes, this study found that LMX does not significantly moderate the link between IWB and performance among millennials in this region ($p = .610$) (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 37).

### Multidimensional Performance
Performance is enhanced across three domains when employees innovate: task performance (core duties), contextual performance (socio-psychological environment), and adaptive performance (reacting to dynamics) (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 34).

## Contradictions

- **DL → Performance Direct Path**: Conflicts with [[farhaq-and-yulianti-2024]] which found a significant direct effect (β = 2.581, p = 0.010), but aligns with Arief Rizqi et al. (2023) (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 37).
- **LMX Moderation**: Conflicts with Jun Chul Ha (2022) findings that LMX significantly impacts organizational performance outcomes (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 38).

## Practical Implications
Organizations should focus digital leadership efforts on stimulating creative outputs rather than expecting direct performance gains. Managers need to understand that for millennials, the path to high performance is paved with the freedom and support to innovate.

## Limitation
The study notes the potential for inconsistent answers due to the use of Likert scales and the time-consuming nature of the questionnaire for some respondents.
