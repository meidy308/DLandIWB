# Bakker & Demerouti (2017) – Job Demands–Resources Theory: Taking Stock and Looking Forward

**Summary**: This article synthesizes the first 15 years of JD-R research, formalizing the model into a mature theory with 8 propositions. It introduces concepts of self-undermining and personal demands and outlines future research directions in multilevel modeling and leadership.

**Sources**: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md

## 8 Propositions of JD-R Theory

| Proposition | Description | Key Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **P1** | All job characteristics can be classified into two categories. | Job Demands vs. Job Resources |
| **P2** | Demands and resources instigate two different processes. | Health-impairment vs. Motivational process |
| **P3** | Job resources buffer the impact of job demands on strain. | Buffer hypothesis |
| **P4** | Job resources boost motivation particularly when demands are high. | Saliency hypothesis |
| **P5** | Personal resources play a similar role as job resources. | Direct/Buffering effects |
| **P6** | Motivation relates positively to performance; strain relates negatively. | Performance link |
| **P7** | Engaged employees use job crafting to create gain spirals. | [[job-crafting]] |
| **P8** | Strained employees use self-undermining to fuel loss spirals. | [[self-undermining]] |

## Key Findings

### Evolution from Model to Theory
The JD-R model was originally used to explain burnout but now accounts for various types of employee well-being and performance (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 273). The theory now explicitly includes the active role of employees in shaping their work environment.

### Self-Undermining and Loss Spirals
Strained employees (e.g., chronically exhausted) create obstacles that undermine performance. They communicate poorly, make mistakes, and create conflicts, which increases their job demands over time (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 277).

### Personal Demands
Introduced as requirements individuals set for themselves (e.g., workaholism, perfectionism) that require effort and have costs (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 279).

### The Role of Leadership
Leaders, particularly those using [[transformational-leadership]], influence the work environment by creating resources (autonomy, support) which help followers deal with challenges (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 280).

## Practical Implications

- **JD-R Monitor**: Interactive electronic questionnaires providing immediate personalized feedback to employees and managers.
- **Job Crafting Interventions**: Training employees to specify personal crafting goals to optimize their own demands and resources.
- **Serious Games**: Simulating managerial actions to show how resource allocation affects performance and stress over time.

## Limitation

- **Direct links**: The theory often treats the health and motivational processes as independent, but empirical studies show cross-paths (e.g., resources affecting burnout) (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 278).
- **Specificity**: The heuristic flexibility of JD-R can lead to ambiguity in categorizing specific job characteristics (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 278).
