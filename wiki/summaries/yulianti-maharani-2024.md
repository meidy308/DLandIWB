# Yulianti and Maharani (2024) – Pengaruh Digital Leadership terhadap Kinerja Karyawan: Peran Mediasi kreativitas dan Perilaku Kerja Inovatif

**Summary**: This study examines the indirect effect of digital leadership on employee performance through creativity and innovative work behavior at PT Semen Indonesia (Persero) Tbk. It finds that innovative work behavior fully mediates the relationship, while creativity alone does not.

**Sources**: yulianti-and-maharani-2024.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Emma Yulianti and Nova Maharani (Fakultas Ekonomi dan Bisnis UHW Perbanas)
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: Jurnal Ekonomi Manajemen Akuntansi, 30(2), 145-164
- **Methodology**: Quantitative (Survey)
- **Sample**: 74 employees, PT Semen Indonesia (Persero) Tbk, Indonesia
- **Analysis**: SEM-PLS (SmartPLS 4.0)
- **Data collection**: Questionnaires, October 2024

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (measured by attitude, digital competency, digital behavior, and digital transformation skills)
- **Dependent variable**: [[employee-performance]] (measured by task, contextual, and adaptive performance)
- **Mediator 1**: [[employee-creativity]] (measured by novelty and utility)
- **Mediator 2**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (measured by idea generation, promotion, and realization)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Not supported** (B = 0.138, T = 1.715, p = 0.086) |
| H2 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.502, T = 5.706, p = 0.000) |
| H3 | [[innovative-work-behavior]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.568, T = 5.106, p = 0.000) |
| H4 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[employee-creativity]] (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.382, T = 3.386, p = 0.001) |
| H5 | [[employee-creativity]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Not supported** (B = 0.212, T = 1.664, p = 0.096) |
| H6 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] → [[employee-performance]] | **Supported (Full Mediation)** (B = 0.285, T = 3.758, p = 0.000) |
| H7 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[employee-creativity]] → [[employee-performance]] | **Not supported** (B = 0.081, T = 1.319, p = 0.187) |

## Key Findings

### Full Mediation of IWB
Digital leadership does not directly influence employee performance in this context. Instead, it works entirely through fostering [[innovative-work-behavior]]. Leaders who demonstrate digital competence and a clear vision for digital transformation encourage employees to generate and realize new ideas, which then drives performance (source: yulianti-and-maharani-2024.md, p. 161).

### The Creativity-Performance Gap
While digital leadership successfully stimulates [[employee-creativity]], this creativity does not automatically translate into higher performance. The authors suggest that creativity is often "potential" that hasn't been implemented yet, whereas IWB includes the "realization" phase which is critical for performance outcomes (source: yulianti-and-maharani-2024.md, p. 161).

### Inconsistency in Direct Effects
The study highlights inconsistencies in previous research regarding the direct effect of digital leadership on performance, citing studies like Muniroh et al. (2022) which also found no direct effect, contrasting with others like Artüz & Bayraktar (2021) (source: yulianti-and-maharani-2024.md, p. 159).

## Practical Implications

- Management should implement digital leadership as a strategy to increase creativity and innovative behavior.
- Fostering an environment where creative ideas are not just generated but also **realized** (as IWB) is crucial for improving employee performance.
- Digital leadership training should focus on digital competency and the ability to articulate a clear digital vision.

## Limitation

- **Cross-sectional data**: The study cannot describe relationships over time.
- **Single method bias**: Data was collected solely through questionnaires, which may lead to perceptual bias.
- **Small sample size**: Only 74 respondents.
