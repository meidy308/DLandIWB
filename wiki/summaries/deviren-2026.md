# Deviren (2026) – The Impact of Digital Leadership and Digital Culture on Innovative Work Behavior and Adaptive Performance

**Summary**: An empirical study examining the effects of digital leadership and digital culture on innovative work behavior and adaptive performance in the Turkish automotive sector, with digital culture as a moderator and IWB as a mediator.

**Sources**: Deviren - 2026.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Sema Deviren (Istanbul Beykent University, PhD Student)
- **Year**: 2026
- **Journal**: Edunorm Journal of Multidisciplinary Academic Research, 2(1), 27–57
- **Methodology**: Quantitative, relational survey design
- **Sample**: 451 employees in the automotive sector (Turkey)
- **Analysis**: PROCESS Macro 4.1 in SPSS 26; CFA in AMOS 24.0
- **Data collection**: Online questionnaires (2024)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (2 subdimensions: innovative & supportive; 9 items; Büyükbeşe et al., 2022)
- **Dependent variable**: [[adaptive-performance]] (unidimensional; 19 items; Charbonnier-Voirin & Roussel, 2012)
- **Mediator**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (unidimensional; 10 items; Jong & den Hartog, adapted by Çimen & Yücel, 2017)
- **Moderator**: [[digital-organizational-culture]] (5 subdimensions: digital intelligence/leadership, adaptation, human capital, technology, collaboration; 23 items; composite scale)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | DL → Adaptive Performance (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.60, p < .01) |
| H2 | DL → IWB (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.80, p < .01) |
| H3 | IWB → Adaptive Performance (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.19, p < .01) |
| H4 | IWB mediates DL → AP | **Supported** (but negative indirect effect, B = −0.03) |
| H5 | DC moderates DL → IWB | **Supported** (negative moderation, B = −0.15, p < .01) |
| H6 | DC moderates DL → AP | **Supported** (negative moderation, B = −0.10, p < .01) |

## Key Findings

### Strong direct effects
- Digital leadership and digital culture together explain **82%** of variance in IWB (R² = 0.82) (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 39).
- Digital leadership, digital culture, and IWB together explain **87%** of variance in adaptive performance (R² = 0.87) (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 39).
- All correlations among the study variables were positive, significant, and strong (p < .01) (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 37–38).

### Negative moderation by digital culture
- Digital culture **negatively moderates** the DL → IWB relationship: as digital culture increases, the effect of digital leadership on IWB decreases (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 39–40).
- Digital culture also **negatively moderates** the DL → AP relationship (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 40).
- This is interpreted as a **substitution effect**: when digital culture is strong, its influence absorbs or replaces the effect that digital leadership would otherwise exert. When digital culture is weak, the effect of digital leadership is substantially stronger (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 42–43).

### Negative mediation by IWB
- IWB has a **significant but negative indirect effect** on the DL → AP relationship (B = −0.03). The direct effect of digital leadership on adaptive performance is substantially higher than the indirect path through IWB (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 43).
- This means that although DL significantly drives both IWB and AP, the pathway from DL through IWB does not additionally boost AP—it slightly reduces the total effect.

### No demographic differences
- Employees' perceptions of DL, DC, IWB, and AP did not significantly differ by gender, age, education level, job title, or tenure (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 43–44).

## Contradictions

- The **negative moderating role** of digital culture contrasts with the **positive mediating role** found in [[cetinkaya-surucu-2025]], where digital organizational culture was a catalyst that supported innovation. The Deviren study suggests that once digital culture is already strong, additional digital leadership has diminishing returns on IWB and AP.
- The **negative mediation** by IWB is also unexpected, as most prior studies (e.g., [[ahmed-et-al-2024]]) position IWB as a positive pathway connecting leadership to performance outcomes.

## Practical Implications

- Organizations should invest in digital leadership development and cultivating a strong digital culture, as both directly drive innovation and performance (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 43).
- Digital transformation initiatives should be developed in a systematic, incremental, and quantifiable manner to avoid the diminishing-returns dynamic observed with the negative moderation (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 44).
