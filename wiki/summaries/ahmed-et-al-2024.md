# Ahmed et al. (2024) – Digital Leadership and Innovative Work Behavior: Impact of LMX, Learning Orientation and Innovation Capabilities

**Summary**: A two-wave longitudinal study examining the impact of digital leadership on innovative work behavior through the sequential mediating roles of learning orientation and innovation capabilities, with leader-member exchange (LMX) as moderator, in the French automobile industry.

**Sources**: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Farooq Ahmed (University of Malaya), M. Muzamil Naqshbandi (University of Brunei Darussalam), Mehwish Waheed (Université Paris-Saclay / IMT-BS), Noor ul Ain (Léonard de Vinci Research Center)
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: Management Decision, 62(11), 3607–3632
- **Methodology**: Quantitative, two-wave longitudinal field survey
- **Sample**: 440 employees and managers in automobile companies in France (Detroit, USA and Paris, France)
- **Analysis**: PLS-SEM via SmartPLS (bootstrapping)
- **Data collection**: Online questionnaires in two phases — Time 1 (Sep–Oct 2022): demographics, digital leadership, LMX; Time 2 (Nov–Dec 2022): learning orientation, innovation capabilities, IWB

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (5 items, 7-point Likert; Artuz & Bayraktar, 2021)
- **Mediator 1**: [[learning-orientation]] (5 items, 7-point Likert; Aboobaker & Zakkariya, 2020)
- **Mediator 2**: [[innovation-capabilities]] (5 items, 3 retained after CFA, 7-point Likert; Benitez et al., 2022)
- **Moderator**: [[leader-member-exchange]] (5 items, 7-point Likert; Erdogan & Enders, 2007)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (5 items, 4 retained after CFA, 7-point Likert; Aboobaker & Zakkariya, 2020)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | DL → Learning Orientation (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.548, t = 10.867, p = 0.000) |
| H2 | LMX moderates DL → Learning Orientation (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.044, t = 3.069, p = 0.002) |
| H3 | Learning Orientation → Innovation Capabilities (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.453, t = 7.586, p = 0.000) |
| H4 | Innovation Capabilities → IWB (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.424, t = 7.058, p = 0.000) |
| H5 | Learning Orientation mediates DL → Innovation Capabilities | **Supported** (β = 0.248, t = 5.016, p = 0.000) |
| H6 | Innovation Capabilities mediates Learning Orientation → IWB | **Supported** (β = 0.192, t = 4.450, p = 0.000) |
| H7 | Sequential mediation: DL → LO → IC → IWB | **Supported** (β = 0.105, t = 3.496, p = 0.001) |

## Key Findings

### Sequential mediation pathway
Digital leadership does not directly impact IWB in this model; instead, the effect follows a serial pathway: DL → Learning Orientation → Innovation Capabilities → IWB. The total indirect effect through this sequential mediation is significant (β = 0.105, p = 0.001) (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3622).

### Moderating role of LMX
The quality of [[leader-member-exchange]] significantly amplifies the DL → learning orientation link. When LMX is high, employees with strong digital leaders develop even stronger learning orientations. Notably, the effect was also significant at low levels of LMX, meaning the relationship strengthens across all levels (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3620–3621).

### Variance explained
- Learning orientation: R² = 0.410 (digital leadership and LMX explain 41% of variance)
- Innovation capabilities: R² = 0.205
- Innovative work behavior: R² = 0.179
(source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3619)

### Theoretical grounding
The study is grounded in [[social-exchange-theory]] (Blau, 1964), which posits that employees evaluate the digital leadership shown by their superiors and reciprocate by enhancing their own learning, building innovation capabilities, and ultimately exhibiting innovative work behavior (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3608–3610).

## Contradictions

- The relatively low R² for IWB (0.179) compared to other studies in the wiki (e.g., Deviren 2026 at 0.87) suggests that the sequential mediation model captures only part of the variance. Other factors not included in this model likely play important roles.
- The study found no significant direct DL → IWB path; the entire effect is mediated. This contrasts with [[erhan-et-al-2022]] and [[deviren-2026]], where digital leadership has strong direct effects on IWB.

## Practical Implications

- Organizations should invest in targeted leadership development programs to build digital leadership competencies and foster quality LMX relationships (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3624–3625).
- Cultivating a learning-oriented culture is a strategic imperative: learning orientation is the gateway through which digital leadership translates into innovation capabilities and ultimately IWB (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3623).
- The automobile industry in France can gain a competitive edge by combining digital leadership development with strategic emphasis on learning orientations and innovation capabilities (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3625).
