# Khan et al. (2025) – Management of green innovation: the influence of digital leadership on employee behavior in an empirical investigation

**Summary**: This study explores how digital leadership fosters green innovative behavior among employees through the development of a green organizational climate, particularly in firms with high strategic agility.

**Sources**: Khan et al. - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Ali Nawaz Khan (Hubei Engineering University), Naseer Abbas Khan (University of Sargodha), Khalid Mehmood (Hubei Engineering University)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Baltic Journal of Management, 20(5), 675-694
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 306 employees, South China-based software firms (Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Dongguan)
- **Analysis**: SEM (using CFA)
- **Data collection**: Online and offline structured questionnaire, 2024

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (Scale: Shin et al., 2023)
- **Dependent variable**: [[green-innovative-behavior]] (Scale: Verma and Singh, 2022)
- **Mediator**: [[green-organizational-climate]] (Scale: Norton et al., 2014)
- **Moderator**: [[strategic-agility]] (Scale: Idris and Al Rubaie, 2013)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Digital Leadership → Green Organizational Climate (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.43, p < .001) |
| H2 | Green Organizational Climate → Green Innovative Behavior (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.59, p < .001) |
| H3 | Green Organizational Climate mediates DL → GIB | **Supported** (β = 0.14, LLCI = 0.05, ULCI = 0.26) |
| H4 | Strategic Agility moderates DL → GOC Relationship | **Supported** (β = 0.36, p < .001) |
| H5 | Strategic Agility moderates the indirect effect (Moderated Mediation) | **Supported** (Index = 0.113, LLCI = 0.039, ULCI = 0.200) |

## Key Findings

### The Role of Digital Leadership in Sustainability
Digital leadership is identified as a critical organizational resource (RBV perspective) that steers culture toward innovation and sustainability (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 3). It goes beyond technology adoption, focusing on mobilizing digital capabilities to achieve comprehensive environmental goals (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 2).

### Mediation via Green Organizational Climate
Digital leadership does not only influence behavior directly but primarily works by building a [[green-organizational-climate]]. This climate provides a platform for psychological safety, encouraging employees to experiment with green innovations (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 6).

### Strategic Agility as an Amplifier
Strategic agility acts as a crucial moderator. The study found that in organizations with low strategic agility, the indirect effect of digital leadership on green innovative behavior was **not significant** (β = 0.051, 95% CI contains zero) (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 11). High agility allows firms to pivot resources quickly toward environmental goals, making leadership efforts more tangible.

## Practical Implications

- **Develop Digital Leadership**: Organizations should train leaders in both digital competencies and environmental management to effectively communicate green initiatives via digital platforms (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 13).
- **Foster Green Climate**: Managers must establish supportive policies, resources, and recognition for environmental achievements before expecting innovation (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 13).
- **Invest in Agility**: Agility is critical to translate leadership vision into behavioral shifts. Firms should implement agile management practices and flexible resource allocation (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 13).

## Limitation

- **Causality**: The cross-sectional design limits definitive causal claims between variables (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 13).
- **Generalizability**: The sample is limited to software firms in South China; results may vary in other cultural or industrial contexts (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 13).
- **Methodological Depth**: The reliance on self-reported questionnaires (even with multi-source approaches) could still be prone to common method bias.
