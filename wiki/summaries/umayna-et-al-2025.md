# Umayna et al. (2025) – Increasing Competitive Advantage with Creativity and Innovation: The Moderating Effect of Digital Leadership

**Summary**: A quantitative study of 254 MSMEs in South Sulawesi, Indonesia, exploring how [[psychological-empowerment]] influences [[competitive-advantage]] through [[problem-solving-creativity]] and [[innovative-work-behavior]], with [[digital-leadership]] acting as a critical moderator that catalyzes these relationships.

**Sources**: Umayna et al. - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Farah Umayna (Universitas Terbuka), Shalahuddin Shalahuddin (Universitas Borneo Tarakan), Dedy Darmawan (STIE Widya Praja), et al.
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Advances in Human Resource Management Research, 3(3), 133-150
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 254 MSMEs, South Sulawesi, Indonesia
- **Analysis**: Structural Equation Modeling using Partial Least Squares (SEM-PLS)
- **Data collection**: Structured questionnaire (Likert 1–5), 2025

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[psychological-empowerment]] (Meaning, competence, self-determination, and impact)
- **Dependent variable**: [[competitive-advantage]] (Differentiation, cost efficiency, innovation-driven responsiveness)
- **Mediator**: [[problem-solving-creativity]] (Generation of original and valuable ideas)
- **Mediator**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Idea generation, promotion, and realization)
- **Moderator**: [[digital-leadership]] (Technological vision and strategic organizational direction)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1a | PE → CR (+) | **Supported** ($\beta$ = 0.413, p = 0.000) |
| H1b | PE → IWB (+) | **Supported** ($\beta$ = 0.253, p = 0.001) |
| H1c | PE → CA (+) | **Not supported** ($\beta$ = 0.124, p = 0.172) |
| H2a | CR → IWB (+) | **Supported** ($\beta$ = 0.367, p = 0.000) |
| H2b | CR → CA (+) | **Not supported** ($\beta$ = 0.122, p = 0.175) |
| H3 | IWB → CA (+) | **Supported** ($\beta$ = 0.230, p = 0.015) |
| H6 | DL → CA (+) | **Supported** ($\beta$ = 0.212, p = 0.006) |

| H4b | PE → CR → CA (+) | **Not supported** (p = 0.193) |

## Key Findings

### The "Innovation-Implementation" Bridge
The study confirms that neither [[psychological-empowerment]] nor [[problem-solving-creativity]] directly leads to [[competitive-advantage]]. Instead, their influence is fully mediated by [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Umayna et al. - 2025.md, p. 139). Creativity provides the cognitive foundation, but behavioral execution (IWB) is required to transform ideas into market value.

### Digital Leadership as a Dynamic Catalyst
[[digital-leadership]] significantly strengthens two critical paths:
1.  **Ideation to Action**: It accelerates the transformation of creativity into actual innovative work behavior ($\beta$ = 0.405) (source: Umayna et al. - 2025.md, p. 140).
2.  **Action to Advantage**: It amplifies the impact of innovative behavior on firm competitiveness ($\beta$ = 0.325) (source: Umayna et al. - 2025.md, p. 140).

### Theoretical Alignment with DCT
Grounded in [[dynamic-capabilities-theory]], the study positions psychological empowerment and IWB as micro-foundations of "sensing" and "seizing," while digital leadership enables "reconfiguring" to maintain strategic alignment with market shifts (source: Umayna et al. - 2025.md, p. 136).

## Practical Implications

- **Focus on Execution**: SME managers should prioritize the *implementation* of ideas (IWB) over just "thinking outside the box."
- **Empowerment as Foundation**: Building a sense of meaning and competence (PE) is the necessary first step to spark creativity and innovation.
- **Invest in Digital Vision**: Developing digital leadership is not optional for MSMEs; it is a strategic imperative to ensure that innovation efforts translate into actual competitive gains.

## Limitation

- **Generalizability**: The sample is limited to MSMEs in South Sulawesi, which may not reflect different regional or industrial contexts in Indonesia (source: Umayna et al. - 2025.md, p. 146).
- **Self-Report Bias**: The study relies on cross-sectional survey data, which could be subject to common method variance.
