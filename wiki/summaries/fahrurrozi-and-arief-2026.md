# Fahrurrozi and Arief (2026) – Digital Leadership, Compensation, Kaizen Culture, and Innovative Work Behavior in Strengthening Employee Loyalty

**Summary**: This study investigates the effects of digital leadership, Kaizen culture, and employee compensation on employee loyalty, with innovative work behavior (IWB) as a mediating variable in an energy infrastructure company. Findings indicate that compensation is the most significant driver of both IWB and loyalty, while digital leadership and Kaizen culture had no significant direct effects in this specific context.

**Sources**: Fahrurrozi and Arief - 2026.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Muhammad Anis Fahrurrozi (Master’s Degree Program in Management at STIE Kusuma Negara, Jakarta), Muhtosim Arief, Dasih Irma
- **Year**: 2026
- **Journal**: Journal of Accounting, Management and Economics Research (JAMER), 4(2), 57-70
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 102 employees, energy infrastructure (PT Kian Santang Muliatama Tbk), Indonesia
- **Analysis**: Partial Least Squares–Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM)
- **Data collection**: Structured questionnaires (5-point Likert scale)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Digital Leadership → IWB (+) | **Not supported** (p = .3195) |
| H2 | Kaizen Culture → IWB (+) | **Not supported** (p = .6969) |
| H3 | Employee Compensation → IWB (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.581, p < .001) |
| H4 | IWB → Employee Loyalty (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.635, p < .001) |
| H5 | Digital Leadership → Employee Loyalty (+) | **Not supported** (p = .261) |
| H6 | Kaizen Culture → Employee Loyalty (+) | **Not supported** (p = .694) |
| H7 | Employee Compensation → Employee Loyalty (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.505, p < .001) |
| H8 | Digital Leadership → IWB → Employee Loyalty | **Not supported** (p = .340) |
| H9 | Kaizen Culture → IWB → Employee Loyalty | **Not supported** (p = .702) |
| H10 | Employee Compensation → IWB → Employee Loyalty | **Supported** (B = 0.369, p = .0002) |

## Key Findings

### Dominance of Compensation
Compensation is identified as the most consistent and influential driver of both innovative behavior and long-term loyalty in the energy sector. It acts as both a direct predictor and an indirect one through IWB (source: Fahrurrozi and Arief - 2026.md, p. 58).

### Insignificance of Digital Leadership and Kaizen Culture
Contrary to many transformational studies, digital leadership and Kaizen culture did not show significant effects on IWB or loyalty in this specific organization. This suggests that in project-based energy sectors, financial security and tangible rewards may outweigh leadership style or continuous improvement culture (source: Fahrurrozi and Arief - 2026.md, p. 65).

### IWB as a Behavioral Mechanism
The study confirms that when employees engage in [[innovative-work-behavior]], they develop a stronger sense of ownership and attachment to the organization, which contributes to [[employee-loyalty]] (source: Fahrurrozi and Arief - 2026.md, p. 66).

## Contradictions

- **Digital Leadership**: Conflicts with [[gao-and-gao-2024]] and other studies that found a significant positive link between digital leadership and IWB.
- **Kaizen Culture**: Conflicts with general Japanese management philosophy claims that Kaizen inherently drives innovation and loyalty (source: Fahrurrozi and Arief - 2026.md, p. 59).

## Practical Implications

- **Prioritize Compensation**: Organizations should allocate 10–15% of annual payroll to variable pay tied to innovation output and project milestones (source: Fahrurrozi and Arief - 2026.md, p. 67).
- **Operationalize Digital Leadership**: Leadership training should be tied to measurable KPIs like digital adoption rates (>80%) and innovation facilitation scores (source: Fahrurrozi and Arief - 2026.md, p. 67).

## Limitation

- **Cross-sectional design**: Limits the ability to capture changes over time.
- **Single-company sample**: Restricts generalizability to other industries or contexts.
- **Self-reported data**: Subject to response bias (source: Fahrurrozi and Arief - 2026.md, p. 68).
