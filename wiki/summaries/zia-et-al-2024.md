# Zia et al. (2024) – Digital job resources, digital engagement, digital leadership, and innovative work behaviour: a serial mediation model

**Summary**: This study develops and tests a serial mediation model where digital job resources (communication and training) influence innovative work behavior through the sequential mechanisms of digital engagement and digital leadership among technological professionals.

**Sources**: Zia et al. - 2024.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Zia Ayesha, Memon Mumtaz Ali, Mirza Muhammad Zeeshan, Iqbal Yasmine Muhammad Javaid, Tariq Adeel
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: European Journal of Innovation Management
- **Methodology**: Quantitative (Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modeling - PLS-SEM)
- **Sample**: 307 technological professionals in Pakistan (full-time employees in the corporate sector)
- **Analysis**: SmartPLS 4.0
- **Data collection**: LinkedIn-based purposive snowball sampling (December 2022 - March 2023)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-communication]] (4-item scale by Na-Nan et al., 2019), [[digital-training]] (5-item scale adapted from Schmidt, 2004)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (4-item scale from Nguyen et al., 2019)
- **Mediator 1**: [[digital-engagement]] (6-item scale adapted from Shuck et al., 2017)
- **Mediator 2**: [[digital-leadership]] (6-item scale from Zeike et al., 2019)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Digital Training → Digital Engagement | **Not supported** ($\beta=0.059, p=0.141$) |
| H2 | Digital Communication → Digital Engagement | **Supported** ($\beta=0.147, p=0.009$) |
| H3 | Digital Engagement → Digital Leadership | **Supported** ($\beta=0.653, p=0.000$) |
| H4 | Digital Leadership → Innovative Work Behavior | **Supported** ($\beta=0.616, p=0.000$) |
| H5a | Digital Training → Digital Engagement → Digital Leadership → IWB | **Not supported** ($\beta=0.024, p=0.296$) |
| H5b | Digital Communication → Digital Engagement → Digital Leadership → IWB | **Supported** ($\beta=0.074, p=0.009$) |

## Key Findings

### Digital Engagement as a Driver of Digital Leadership
The study finds that digital engagement is a crucial prerequisite for nurturing digital leadership capabilities (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 12). This reverses the typical model where leadership drives engagement, suggesting that deep immersion in digital tools allows employees to hone strategic thinking and foresight necessary for leadership roles.

### The Role of Digital Communication
Digital communication acts as a primary organizational resource that fosters collaboration and information retrieval, which significantly boosts digital engagement among professionals (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 12).

### The Ineffectiveness of Routine Digital Training
Contrary to expectations, digital training did not significantly impact digital engagement. This is attributed to technological professionals perceiving routine training as repetitive or a mere formality that adds to their workload without providing tangible benefits (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 13).

### Serial Mediation Mechanism
The relationship between digital communication and innovative work behavior is serially mediated by digital engagement and digital leadership (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 14).

## Practical Implications

- **Enhance Communication**: Organizations should foster an environment of open dialogue and transparent communication using user-friendly digital platforms (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 14).
- **Targeted Training**: Managers should move away from generic digital training and instead conduct Systematic Training Needs Analysis (TNA) to provide tailored skill development (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 15).
- **Nurture Digital Leadership**: Identify employees with high digital engagement and provide them with opportunities to lead digital initiatives.

## Limitation

- **Sample Specificity**: The study focuses only on technological professionals; results might vary in industries with lower technology integration (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 15).
- **Methodology**: Relies on self-reported survey data, which could be subject to common method bias (though statistically tested and found not to be a major concern) (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 15).
