# Li & Xiao (2023) – Research on the Effect of E-Leadership on Employee Innovation Behavior in the Context of “Self” and “Relationship”

**Summary**: This study develops a dual-mediation model to explain how E-leadership (digital leadership) drives innovation through both the individual's "Self" (Psychological Capital) and "Relationship" (Affective Commitment to Leadership) perspectives, while identifying power distance as a critical negative moderator.

**Sources**: Li and Xiao - 2023.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Min Li and Weihao Xiao (Jiangxi University of Finance and Economics)
- **Year**: 2023
- **Journal**: Journal of Organizational and End User Computing, 35(1), 1–20
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 301 staff and managers from digital enterprises in Fujian, Jiangxi, and Sichuan, China
- **Analysis**: Hierarchical regression analysis and Bootstrapping (SPSS 22.0)
- **Data collection**: Anonymous questionnaire survey (June–July 2022)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (referred to as E-leadership; measured using Liu 2007 scale focusing on ICT-mediated influence)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (referred to as Employee Innovation Behavior; measured via Scott & Bruce 1994 scale)
- **Mediator**: 
    - [[psychological-capital]] ("Self" perspective; optimism, hope, resilience, and self-efficacy)
    - [[affective-commitment-to-leadership]] ("Relationship" perspective; emotional identification with the leader)
- **Moderator**: [[power-distance]] (individual cultural value orientation)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | E-leadership → Psychological Capital (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.356, p < .01) |
| H2 | PsyCap mediates E-leadership → Innovation Behavior | **Supported** |
| H3 | E-leadership → Affective Commitment to Leadership (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.251, p < .01) |
| H4 | Affective Commitment mediates E-leadership → Innovation Behavior | **Supported** |
| H5a | Power Distance moderates E-leadership → PsyCap (-) | **Supported** (B = -0.116, p < .05) |
| H5b | Power Distance moderates E-leadership → Affective Commitment (-) | **Supported** (B = -0.154, p < .01) |
| H6a | Power Distance moderates mediating effect of PsyCap (-) | **Supported** |
| H6b | Power Distance moderates mediating effect of Affective Commitment (-) | **Supported** |

## Key Findings

### The Dual Pathway of Digital Leadership
The study distinguishes between two psychological mechanisms through which digital leaders foster innovation (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 16):
- **Perspective of "Self"**: By utilizing digital media to communicate care and support, leaders increase employees' psychological capital stock—including self-efficacy, optimism, hope, and resilience. This internal store of psychological resources motivates employees to pursue self-actualization through innovative acts.
- **Perspective of "Relationship"**: Digital leadership enhances interaction quality and satisfy personal emotional requirements, leading to higher affective commitment to the leader. Based on [[social-exchange-theory]], employees repay this high-quality relationship by engaging in extra-role behaviors such as innovation.

### The Boundary Role of Power Distance
Cultural values significantly impact how employees receive digital leadership signals (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 16-17):
- **Low Power Distance**: Employees are more sensitive to digital communication and feel closer to their leaders, which amplifies the positive impact of digital leadership on both PsyCap and commitment.
- **High Power Distance**: Employees maintain a greater psychological distance and believe more in authority/obedience. They are less sensitive to digital "caring" signals, which weakens the transmission of leadership influence to psychological states and behaviors.

## Practical Implications

- **Innovate Leadership Concepts**: Leaders in the information era should shift away from traditional pyramid command modes and focus on mastering digital management communication and thinking (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 17).
- **Monitor Psychological States**: Organizations should use digital platforms to promptly address employee needs and address positive praise, thereby building the "psychological capital" necessary for innovation (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 17).
- **Foster Open Culture**: To mitigate the dampening effect of high power distance, leaders should use digital channels to encourage staff to express opinions freely and contribute creative ideas (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 17).

## Limitation

- **Cross-sectional Data**: The study uses data from a single point in time, which limits the ability to track dynamic shifts in variables over time (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 18).
- **Sampling Scope**: While covering several provinces in China, the findings may benefit from broader geographical or multi-cultural validation.
- **Critical Analysis**: The study relies on self-reported questionnaires filled out simultaneously for all variables, potentially introducing common method bias, although the authors claims the use of anonymous filling and mixed item presentation as countermeasures.
