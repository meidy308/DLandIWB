# Fauziah et al. (2026) – Enhancing Employee Innovation Through Digital Talent Development: The Mediation of Digital Competency, Leadership and Culture in the Telecom Industry

**Summary**: This study investigates how digital talent development influences innovative work behavior through the mediating roles of digital leadership, competency, and culture, primarily finding that talent development has no significant direct impact on innovation without these mediators.

**Sources**: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Nadira Aribah Fauziah, Anggraini Sukmawati, Bagus Sartono (IPB University)
- **Year**: 2026
- **Journal**: Dinasti International Journal of Economics, Finance and Accounting, 7(1), 475-486
- **Methodology**: Quantitative (SEM-PLS)
- **Sample**: 189 certified digital talents, PT Telkom Indonesia
- **Analysis**: Structural Equation Modeling–Partial Least Squares (SEM-PLS) using SmartPLS 4 and IBM SPSS Statistics 26
- **Data collection**: Online questionnaire (June to August 2025)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-talent-development]] (measured by indicators like training, upskilling, and reskilling)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (measured using scale from De Jong & Den Hartog, 2010)
- **Mediator**: 
	- [[digital-leadership]] (innovation-oriented cognition and social capital)
	- [[digital-competency]] (knowledge, skills, and attitudes in utilizing digital resources)
	- [[digital-organizational-culture]] (collaboration, openness to ideas, and support for experimentation)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Digital talent development → IWB (+) | **Not supported** (B = 0.076, p = 0.202) |
| H2 | Digital talent development → Digital leadership → IWB | **Supported** (B = 0.095, p = 0.047) |
| H3 | Digital talent development → Digital competency → IWB | **Supported** (B = 0.094, p = 0.036) |
| H4 | Digital talent development → Digital culture → IWB | **Supported** (B = 0.239, p = 0.000) |
| H5 | DTD → DL * DC * DCu → IWB | **Supported** (B = 0.428, p = 0.000) |

## Key Findings

### Mediation as a Strategic Necessity
The study demonstrates that innovative behavior among digital talent is not solely shaped by individual-level factors (like being "trained"), but is strongly influenced by digitally oriented leadership, competence, and organizational culture (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483).

### Lack of Direct Impact
The direct effect of digital talent development on innovative work behavior was not statistically significant. This suggests that "companies cannot rely solely on digital talent development programs" to drive innovation (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 481).

### Role of Digital Culture
Digital culture was found to be a strong mediator ($B = 0.239, p = 0.000$), establishing a supportive environment for innovation through collaboration, openness to change, and adaptability (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483).

### Integrated Enablers
The simultaneous mediation of leadership, competency, and culture ($B = 0.428$) shows that talent development provides the "groundwork," leadership provides "direction and trust," competency enhances "problem-solving," and culture cultivates the "climate for experimentation" (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483).

## Practical Implications

- Organizations should not confine talent development programs to technical upskilling alone.
- Talent development must be integrated with visionary leadership, adaptive culture, and strong digital competencies to ensure sustainable innovation.
- In the telecom industry, digital readiness requires systematic alignment of organizational enablers to translate investments into actual employee behavior.

## Limitation

- The study relied on a specific sample from PT Telkom Indonesia, which may limit generalizability to other sectors or smaller organizations.
- The use of non-probability purposive sampling may introduce selection bias.
- The cross-sectional design prevents definitively establishing causality over time.
