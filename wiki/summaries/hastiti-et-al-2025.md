# Hastiti et al. (2025) – Digital Leadership in Driving Work Innovation: The Role of Creativity, Motivation, and Gender

**Summary**: A quantitative study of 117 lecturers and staff at Indonesian private universities exploring how [[digital-leadership]] influences innovation solely through psychological mediation, specifically [[intrinsic-motivation]], while highlighting gender differences in these developmental paths.

**Sources**: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Anisa Permata Hastiti, Didik Subiyanto, Syamsul Hadi (Master of Management, Sarjanawiyata Tamansiswa University)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Journal The Winners, 26(1), 43-57
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 117 participants (Lecturers and education staff), private universities in Central Java, Indonesia
- **Analysis**: Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) using AMOS
- **Data collection**: Structured questionnaire (Likert 1–5), data gathered in late 2024/early 2025

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (5 indicators: technology usage, motivation for adoption, data-driven decision making)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (6 indicators: initiation of change, implementation of new ideas)
- **Mediator**: [[intrinsic-motivation]] (5 indicators: job satisfaction, motivation to achieve)
- **Mediator**: [[problem-solving-creativity]] (6 indicators: generation of new ideas, innovative approaches)
- **Moderator**: Gender (Modeled as a moderation perspective via split-sample SEM)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | DL → IWB (+) | **Not supported** (est = -1.088, p = 0.664) |
| H2 | DL → CPS (+) | **Supported** (est = 0.831, p < 0.001) |
| H3 | DL → IM (+) | **Supported** (est = 0.802, p < 0.001) |
| H4 | CPS → IWB (+) | **Not supported** (est = 1.847, p = 0.546) |
| H5 | IM → IWB (+) | **Supported** (est = 0.270, p = 0.009) |

## Key Findings

### The Indirect Nature of Digital Leadership
In the context of private universities, digital leadership does not directly trigger innovation. Instead, it serves as a strategic catalyst that builds internal psychological states (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 53).

### The Essential Role of Intrinsic Motivation
[[intrinsic-motivation]] is the primary engine for converting leadership support into actual innovative output. Without the internal drive to act out of satisfaction and personal goals, digital leadership fails to manifest as IWB (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 52).

### The Creativity-Implementation Gap
While digital leadership successfully fosters [[problem-solving-creativity]], this study confirms that "thinking creatively" does not automatically lead to "working innovatively." Organizational barriers, resource limitations, or resistance to change may prevent creative ideas from being implemented (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 51).

### Gendered Mechanisms of Innovation
Applying [[social-role-theory]], the study reveals significant differences in how genders respond to digital leadership:
- **Women**: Show a significantly stronger path from DL to [[intrinsic-motivation]] (est = 0.902) than men (est = 0.770) (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 52-53).
- **Men**: Exhibit a stronger path from DL to [[problem-solving-creativity]] (est = 0.890) than women (est = 0.761) (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 52).

## Practical Implications

- **Motivation-Centric Leadership**: Institutions should move beyond providing digital tools and focus on building a work culture that strengthens intrinsic satisfaction and autonomy.
- **Gender-Responsive Strategies**: Leadership development programs should be tailored to demographic characteristics, recognizing that women may benefit more from motivational support while men may need more help translating creativity into practical innovation.

## Limitations

- **Sample Size**: N=117 is relatively small for complex SEM analysis, though it meets minimum scientific standards (Gozali & Latan, 2015) (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 46, 51).
- **Sector Specificity**: The results might be unique to the higher education environment where intrinsic motivation for research and teaching is already a high baseline.
- **Cross-sectional Design**: The causality is inferred theoretically but not proven longitudinally.
