# Hastiti et al. (2025)

**Summary**: A quantitative study of 117 lecturers and staff exploring the indirect influence of digital leadership on innovative work behavior through intrinsic motivation and problem-solving creativity, and analyzing the moderating role of gender.

**Sources**: raw/Hastiti et al. - 2025.md

---

## Key Findings

- **Indirect Influence**: [[digital-leadership]] does not have a significant direct effect on [[innovative-work-behavior]] (IWB), but instead drives it indirectly.
- **Intrinsic Motivation**: Digital leadership has a significant positive effect on [[intrinsic-motivation]], which in turn significantly predicts IWB. 
- **Problem-Solving Creativity**: While digital leadership positively enhances [[problem-solving-creativity]], this creativity does not have a significant direct path to IWB.
- **Gender Moderation**: Utilizing [[social-role-theory]], the study shows the positive effect of digital leadership on intrinsic motivation and problem-solving creativity is significantly stronger for women than for men.
