# Afif and Fitrianingrum (2025) – The Effect of Digital Leadership, Work Engagement, and Organizational Experience on Innovative Work Behavior among Generation Z Workers

**Summary**: An empirical study analyzing the effects of digital leadership, work engagement, and organizational experience on innovative work behavior among Generation Z workers with organizational experience in Central Java.

**Sources**: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Sabrina Nabilla Afif & Agustina Fitrianingrum (Universitas Islam Sultan Agung)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Arzusin: Jurnal Manajemen dan Pendidikan Dasar, 5(6), 3108–3127
- **Methodology**: Quantitative, explanatory research with survey model
- **Sample**: 271 respondents, Generation Z workers with organizational experience in Central Java, Indonesia
- **Analysis**: PLS-SEM via SmartPLS (bootstrapping)
- **Data collection**: Online questionnaire (Google Form), purposive sampling (2025)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (3 indicators: technology integration, adaptability, innovation; 5-point Likert)
- **Independent variable**: [[work-engagement]] (3 indicators: vigor, dedication, absorption; 5-point Likert)
- **Independent variable**: [[organization-experience]] (3 indicators: framing, co-creating, connecting; 5-point Likert)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (3 indicators: idea generation, idea promotion, idea realization; 5-point Likert)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Digital Leadership → IWB (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.153, t = 2.282, p = 0.023) |
| H2 | Work Engagement → IWB (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.262, t = 3.805, p = 0.000) |
| H3 | Organization Experience → IWB (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.412, t = 5.759, p = 0.000) |

## Key Findings

### Variance explained
- The three IVs together explain **50.1%** of the variance in IWB (R² Adjusted = 0.501) (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3117).
- The model has good predictive relevance (Q² = 0.357) (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3118).

### Effect sizes
- [[organization-experience]] has the strongest effect on IWB (F² = 0.170, moderate effect), confirming that accumulated organizational knowledge, decision-making courage, and collaboration skills are the most dominant drivers of innovation (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3119–3120).
- [[work-engagement]] has a small-to-moderate effect (F² = 0.082). Individuals who are emotionally connected to their work maximize their capabilities, which is reflected in innovative contributions (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3119).
- [[digital-leadership]] has a weak but significant effect (F² = 0.032). A visionary digital leader serves as an initial trigger that encourages subordinates to experiment and innovate, though its direct effect is comparatively smaller than the other two variables (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3119).

### Theoretical grounding
The study is grounded in [[job-demands-resources-theory]] and [[conservation-of-resources-theory]], positing that digital leadership and organizational experience function as job resources that enhance engagement and drive innovative behavior. The [[communication-as-constitutive-of-organization]] perspective is used to explain how organizational experience shapes meaning, interaction patterns, and adaptive capabilities (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3109–3110).

## Contradictions

- The relatively weak effect of digital leadership (F² = 0.032) contrasts with studies like [[erhan-et-al-2022]] and [[deviren-2026]], where digital leadership has much stronger effects. This may reflect the study's focus on Gen Z workers whose digital nativity reduces the marginal impact of digital leadership.

## Practical Implications

- Organizations should strengthen digital leadership capacities through leader development in technology utilization and change management (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3121).
- Enhance work engagement by creating supportive work environments, granting autonomy, and appreciating innovative contributions (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3121).
- Leverage organizational experience as a strategic asset in recruitment and employee development, especially for driving sustainable innovation (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3121).
