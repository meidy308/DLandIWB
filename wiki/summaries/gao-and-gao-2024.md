# Gao and Gao (2024) – How Does Digital Leadership Foster Employee Innovative Behavior: A Cognitive–Affective Processing System Perspective

**Summary**: This study investigates the dual-path mechanism (cognitive and affective) through which digital leadership influences employee innovative behavior, using the Cognitive–Affective Processing System (CAPS) framework. It finds that psychological empowerment is a critical mediator, while affective commitment and proactive personality play less significant roles in this specific context.

**Sources**: Gao and Gao - 2024.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Pengbin Gao (Harbin Institute of Technology) & Yinghui Gao
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: Behavioral Sciences, 14(5), 362
- **Methodology**: Quantitative (Cross-sectional)
- **Sample**: 359 employees across various industries (Manufacturing, IT, Finance, etc.) in China.
- **Analysis**: Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) using AMOS 27.0 and SmartPLS 3.2.9.
- **Data collection**: Nov 2022 (Pilot) and Jan–April 2023 (Main Study).

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (6-item scale, source: Zeike et al., 2019)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (6-item scale, source: Scott and Bruce, 1994)
- **Mediator**: [[psychological-empowerment]] (12-item scale; Mean, Competence, Self-determination, Impact; source: Spreitzer, 1995)
- **Mediator**: [[affective-commitment]] (6-item scale, source: Allen and Meyer, 1990)
- **Moderator**: [[proactive-personality]] (10-item scale, source: Seibert et al., 1999)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Digital Leadership → Innovative Behavior (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.357, p < .001$ total) |
| H2 | Digital Leadership → Psychological Empowerment (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.374, p < .001$) |
| H3 | PE mediates DL → Innovative Behavior | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.108, p = .001$) |
| H4 | Digital Leadership → Affective Commitment (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.216, p = .001$) |
| H5 | AC mediates DL → Innovative Behavior | **Not supported** ($\beta = 0.033, p = .070$) |
| H6a/b | Proactive Personality moderates DL → PE path | **Not supported** ($\beta = 0.114, p = .093$) |
| H7a/b | Proactive Personality moderates DL → AC path | **Not supported** ($\beta = 0.100, p = .095$) |

## Key Findings

### Digital Leadership as a Driver of Innovation
Digital leadership directly and positively predicts employee innovative behavior. By articulating a digital vision and acting as a role model, digital leaders reduce resistance to technological change and encourage experimentation (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 5).

### The Dominance of the Cognitive Path (Psychological Empowerment)
Psychological empowerment serves as a verified cognitive mechanism. Digital leaders empower employees by enhancing their sense of competence and autonomy in a fast-paced digital environment, which directly motivates them to seek out new technologies and ideas (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 16).

### The Non-Significant Affective Path
Although digital leadership increases affective commitment (emotional attachment to the organization), this commitment did not significantly translate into innovative behavior in this study. This suggests that "feeling good" about the company is less critical for innovation than "feeling capable and autonomous" in a digital context (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 16).

### Proactive Personality Boundary Conditions
Unexpectedly, proactive personality did not significantly moderate the relationships. The authors posit that highly proactive individuals may already possess high intrinsic drive and psychological empowerment, making them less dependent on leadership cues to innovate (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 18).

## Practical Implications
- **Focus on Empowerment**: Organizations should train digital leaders to not only be tech-savvy but also to delegate authority and provide meaningful digital work to enhance psychological empowerment.
- **Strategic Vision**: Leaders must communicate a clear digital strategy to help employees overcome the uncertainty of transformation (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 21).

## Limitation
- **Cross-Sectional Design**: The study uses data from a single point in time after the pilot, which may limit causal inferences compared to longitudinal designs (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 22).
- **Self-Reported Data**: Use of self-reports for all variables (though common) can lead to common method variance, although the study tested for this and found it was not significant (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 14).
