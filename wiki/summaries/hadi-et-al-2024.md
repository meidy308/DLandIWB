# Hadi et al. (2024) – The Effect of Digital Leadership and Organizational Support on Innovative Work Behavior: The Mediating Role of Emotional Intelligence

**Summary**: This study analyzes how digital leadership and organizational support influence innovative work behavior (IWB) through the mediating role of emotional intelligence among art lecturers in Indonesia. It finds that while organizational support and emotional intelligence are significant predictors of IWB, digital leadership has no significant direct or indirect impact in this specific context.

**Sources**: Hadi et al. - 2023.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Syamsul Hadi (Universitas Sarjanawiyata Tamansiswa), Lina Setiawati, Kusuma Chandra Kirana, Suddin Bin Lada, Christina Heti Tri Rahmawati
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: Quality: Access to Success, 25(199), 74–83
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 119 lecturers, Faculty of Fine Arts, Institut Seni Indonesia (ISI) Yogyakarta, Indonesia
- **Analysis**: Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) using SmartPLS 3.2.9
- **Data collection**: Questionnaire (saturated sample/census method)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (Technological skill, Communication, Social skills, Team building, Change management, Trustworthiness; derived from Ajabar et al., 2021)
- **Independent variable**: [[organizational-support]] (Awards, Development, Working conditions, Welfare; derived from Umihastanti & Frianto, 2022)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Idea exploration, generation, championing, and implementation; derived from Erhan et al., 2022)
- **Mediator**: [[emotional-intelligence]] (Self-awareness, self-management, self-motivation, empathy, and relation-management; derived from Setiadi & Tampubolon, 2020)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Not supported** (t = 0.678, p = 0.498) |
| H2 | [[organizational-support]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Supported** (t = 2.747, p = 0.006) |
| H3 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[emotional-intelligence]] (+) | **Not supported** (t = 1.060, p = 0.289) |
| H4 | [[organizational-support]] → [[emotional-intelligence]] (+) | **Supported** (t = 3.74, p = 0.000) |
| H5 | [[emotional-intelligence]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Supported** (t = 8.177, p = 0.000) |

## Key Findings

### Non-Impact of Digital Leadership
Digital leadership was not found to significantly influence IWB or emotional intelligence (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 80). The authors suggest this may be due to the specific demographics (e.g., 21% of staff with <1 year tenure) who may not yet be familiar with digital bureaucratic innovation processes (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 80).

### Role of Organizational Support
Organizational support has real implications for creative ideas and transformation acceleration. Higher perceived support correlates with higher IWB ($p = 0.006$) and higher emotional intelligence ($p = 0.000$) (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 81).

### Emotional Intelligence as a Driver of IWB
Emotional intelligence is a significant predictor of IWB ($p = 0.000$). The study suggests that high emotional intelligence allows lecturers to effectively manage stress and collaborate, facilitating the generation and implementation of new ideas (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 81).

## Contradictions
- **Digital Leadership & IWB**: This study contradicts [[erhan-et-al-2022]] and [[gao-and-gao-2024]], which found positive and significant effects.
- **Leadership & Emotional Intelligence**: This study contradicts findings by Pambudy & Handayati, Acosta-Prado et al., and Ayalew & Ayenew, but supports [[riski-and-riana-2018]] (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 81).

## Practical Implications
- Organizations should focus on increasing employee emotional intelligence through training, coaching, and mentoring to boost IWB (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 81).
- Strengthening organizational infrastructure and support (facilities, awards, and welfare) is more effective for driving innovation in this context than digital leadership alone.

## Limitation
- The authors state the sample was limited to one institution (ISI Yogyakarta) and recommend future comparative research between state and private institutions.
- Critically, the study identifies a "digitalization discomfort" among employees where technology acceleration may cause anxiety, but the digital leadership style measured was unable to mitigate this in the current sample.
