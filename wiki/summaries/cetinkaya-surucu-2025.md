# Çetinkaya and Sürücü (2025) – The Influence of Digital Culture and Digital Leadership on Innovative Work Behavior

**Summary**: A study exploring how digital leadership influences innovative work behavior through the mediating role of digital organizational culture within the IT sector in Istanbul.

**Sources**: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Bülent Çetinkaya (European Leadership University), Lütfi Sürücü (World Peace University)
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Studies in Media and Communication, 13(3), 236–247
- **Methodology**: Quantitative, cross-sectional survey
- **Sample**: 404 employees in IT and technology companies in Istanbul, Turkey
- **Analysis**: SEM via AMOS 22, mediation via PROCESS Macro (Hayes, 2018) with 5000 bootstrapping resamples and 95% CI; SPSS 27 for descriptive statistics
- **Data collection**: Paper questionnaires (sealed envelopes), purposive sampling

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (6 items, 5-point Likert; Zeike et al., 2019)
- **Mediator**: [[digital-organizational-culture]] (4 items, 5-point Likert; Zhen et al., 2021)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (9 items, 5-point Likert; Janssen, 2000)
- **Control variables**: Gender, working position, tenure

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | DL → IWB (+) | **Supported** (β = 0.281, t = 6.125, CI [0.201, 0.345]) |
| H2 | DOC mediates DL → IWB | **Supported** (indirect effect β = 0.144, CI [0.088, 0.216]) |

## Key Findings

### Direct effects
- Digital leadership has a significant positive effect on digital organizational culture (β = 0.452, t = 7.851, CI [0.247, 0.401]) (source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 242).
- Digital leadership has a significant positive direct effect on innovative work behavior (β = 0.281, t = 6.125, CI [0.201, 0.345]) (source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 242).
- Digital organizational culture has a significant positive effect on innovative work behavior (β = 0.213, t = 5.641, CI [0.199, 0.237]) (source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 242).

### Mediation
- Digital organizational culture significantly mediates the DL → IWB relationship (indirect effect β = 0.144, CI [0.088, 0.216]). This means that digital leaders shape a supportive, technologically agile culture, which in turn catalyzes employees to generate practical innovations (source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 242–243).

### Correlations
- DL ↔ DOC: r = 0.452 (p < 0.05)
- DL ↔ IWB: r = 0.348 (p < 0.05)
- DOC ↔ IWB: r = 0.321 (p < 0.05)
(source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 241)

### Model fit
The measurement model showed good fit indices: χ²/df = 2.897, CFI = 0.954, GFI = 0.948, TLI = 0.961, RMSEA = 0.047 (source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 241).

## Contradictions

- The **positive mediating role** of digital organizational culture in this study contrasts with the **negative moderating role** found in [[deviren-2026]], where a strong digital culture diminishes the marginal effect of digital leadership on IWB (substitution effect). These are different statistical roles (mediator vs. moderator), but they present contrasting narratives about how culture interacts with leadership.

## Practical Implications

- Organizations should invest in developing digital leadership capabilities at all levels, emphasizing not just technical proficiency but also emotional intelligence, strategic thinking, and change management (source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 243).
- Cultivating a digital culture that values experimentation, collaboration, and transparency is essential — viewing failure as a learning opportunity unleashes creative potential (source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 242–243).
- Leadership development programs and cultural transformation initiatives should be aligned to reinforce each other for maximum innovation impact (source: Çetinkaya and Sürücü - 2025.md, p. 243–244).
