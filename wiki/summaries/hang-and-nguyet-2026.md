# Hang and Nguyệt (2026) – Digital Leadership and Retail Salesperson Performance

**Summary**: This study examines how digital leadership influences retail salesperson performance through the sequential mediation of digital literacy and innovative work behavior, while identifying digital literacy as a significant moderator.

**Sources**: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Tran Thi Bich Hang (Thuongmai University) and Nguyen Thi My Nguyet (Thuongmai University)
- **Year**: 2026
- **Journal**: International Journal of Asian Business and Information Management, 16(1), 1-23
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 555 salespeople, retail sector, Vietnam (Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City)
- **Analysis**: PLS-SEM (SmartPLS 4.0)
- **Data collection**: Online and in-person surveys (October–November 2024)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (measured through leadership's role in awareness, ethical behavior, and experience sharing; scale from Sow & Aborbie, 2018)
- **Dependent variable**: [[employee-performance]] (measured as "Work Performance"; scale modified from Koopmans et al., 2012)
- **Mediator**: [[digital-literacy]] (scale from Law et al., 2018) and [[innovative-work-behavior]] (scale from Santoso et al., 2019)
- **Moderator**: [[digital-literacy]]

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[digital-literacy]] (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.678, p = .000$) |
| H2 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.353, p = .000$) |
| H3 | [[digital-leadership]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.203, p = .000$) |
| H4 | [[digital-literacy]] → [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.535, p = .000$) |
| H5 | [[digital-literacy]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.389, p = .000$) |
| H6 | [[innovative-work-behavior]] → [[employee-performance]] (+) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.349, p = .000$) |
| H7 | DL → DLit → IWB → Performance (Sequential Mediation) | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.127, p = .000$) |
| H8a | DLit moderates DL → IWB | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.085, p = .000$) |
| H8b | DLit moderates IWB → Performance | **Not supported** ($\beta = -0.042, p = .180$) |
| H8c | DLit moderates DL → Performance | **Supported** ($\beta = 0.081, p = .010$) |

## Key Findings

### Direct Effects
Digital leadership has significant positive direct effects on digital literacy, IWB, and salesperson performance (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 1). However, the IPMA results indicate that while digital leadership has the highest performance in terms of implementation, it has a lower effect coefficient (importance) compared to digital literacy and IWB (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 15).

### Mediation Mechanisms
The study validates a sequential mediation chain: Digital leaders enhance employees' **[[digital-literacy]]**, which fosters **[[innovative-work-behavior]]**, ultimately leading to improved **[[employee-performance]]** (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 16).

### Moderation Roles
Digital literacy serves as a "booster" for the influence of digital leadership. High digital literacy strengthens the relationship between leadership and both innovation behavior and actual work performance (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 16).

### Contextual Barriers
The non-significant moderation of digital literacy on the IWB-Performance path is attributed to the relatively low average level of digital literacy among retail employees in the sample, preventing this factor from functioning as a regulatory mechanism at that stage (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 17).

## Contradictions

The non-significant moderation of digital literacy between IWB and Performance contradicts findings from Santoso et al. (2019) but aligns with Amankona et al. (2025) (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 17).

## Practical Implications

Retail managers should:
1.  Establish a clear digital vision and governance framework.
2.  Prioritize digital leadership training programs for senior and middle management.
3.  Focus recruitment on digitally savvy candidates or invest heavily in digital literacy training (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 17).
4.  Foster an experimental environment where sales personnel feel autonomous in presenting novel digital concepts (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 17).

## Limitation

The convenience sample of 555 salespeople from major Vietnamese cities limits generalizability to other regions or sectors. The study also relies solely on salesperson self-evaluations without incorporating managerial assessments, which might introduce bias (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 18).
