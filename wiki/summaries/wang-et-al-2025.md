# Wang et al. (2025) – Digital leadership and employee innovative performance: the role of job crafting and person–job fit

**Summary**: This study examines how digital leadership promotes employee innovative performance through job crafting, moderated by person-job fit, within the manufacturing sector. It identifies task and cognitive crafting as key mediators, while highlighting the boundary condition of person-job fit in enhancing these effects.

**Sources**: Wang et al. - 2025.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Yongkang Wang, Jonghyuk Park, and Qi Gao
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Psychology, 16:1492264
- **Methodology**: Quantitative (Two-wave survey)
- **Sample**: 306 employees from manufacturing firms in Shandong, China
- **Analysis**: Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM) using SmartPLS 4.1 and Mplus 8.0
- **Data collection**: Two stages (T1 and T2) with a one-month interval (March–April 2024)

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (17-item scale by Wang et al. 2022 / Zhu 2015)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Referred to as Employee Innovative Performance; measured using Janssen's 2000 10-item scale)
- **Mediator**: [[job-crafting]] (Task, Cognitive, and Relational dimensions; measured using Slemp and Vella-Brodrick's 2013 15-item scale)
- **Moderator**: [[person-job-fit]] (Measured using Cable and DeRue's 2002 6-item scale)
- **Theoretical Framework**: [[proactive-motivation-model]]

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Digital Leadership → Innovative Performance (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.188, p < .001) |
| H2a | DL → Task Crafting → Innovative Performance (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.082, p < .001) |
| H2b | DL → Cognitive Crafting → Innovative Performance (+) | **Supported** (B = 0.148, p < .001) |
| H2c | DL → Relational Crafting → Innovative Performance (+) | **Not supported** (B = 0.004, p = 0.670) |
| H3b | PJF moderates Cognitive Crafting → Innovative Performance | **Supported** (B = 0.111, p < .05) |
| H4b | PJF moderates the indirect effect (DL → CC → EIP) | **Supported** (B = 0.062, p < .01) |

## Key Findings

### Digital Leadership and Performance
Digital leadership is positively related to employee innovative performance. Leaders who cultivate a digital culture and allocate resources effectively stimulate innovative behavior (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 10).

### The Mediating Role of Job Crafting
- **Task and Cognitive Crafting**: Both dimensions significantly mediate the impact of digital leadership on innovation. Employees under digital leaders proactively adjust task content and redefine the meaning of their work, which fosters intrinsic motivation (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 10).
- **Relational Crafting**: This dimension did not mediate the relationship. The authors suggest this may be due to the highly centralized and controlled communication structures inherent in manufacturing firms (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 13).

### Moderating Effect of Person-Job Fit
Person-job fit strengthens the positive impact of cognitive crafting on innovation performance. When employees perceive a high congruence between their work and personal perceptions, the motivational effects of cognitive crafting are amplified (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 11).

## Contradictions

- **Relational Crafting**: Unlike some previous studies (e.g., Tuan 2018), this study found no significant effect for relational crafting, highlighting the role of organizational context (manufacturing) in constraining social boundary-crossing (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 13).

## Practical Implications

- **Digital Literacy**: Managers should improve their digital literacy to foster an innovative atmosphere.
- **Support for Job Crafting**: Organizations should create flexible work environments that allow for task autonomy and encourage employees to cognitively redefine their work roles.
- **Fit Alignment**: Ongoing training and development are essential to help employees maintain high levels of alignment with evolving digital job roles.

## Limitation

- **Self-Report Bias**: Although two-wave data was collected, all variables were self-reported, which may still involve common method variance.
- **Context Specificity**: The findings are limited to the manufacturing sector in China.
- **Process vs. Outcome**: The use of Janssen's (2000) scale emphasizes the process of innovative behavior rather than objective innovation outcomes like patent outputs.
