# Huang et al. (2026) – Digital Leadership and Employee Innovation: A Sequential Mediation Model of Job Crafting and Innovation Efficacy

**Summary**: This study investigates how digital leadership influences employee innovation through the sequential mediation of job crafting and innovation efficacy, with organizational innovation climate as a key moderator.

**Sources**: Huang et al. - 2026.md

---

## Bibliographic Details

- **Author**: Meijiao Huang, Rongrong Zhao, and Xiaoxia Chen (Fujian Business University, China)
- **Year**: 2026
- **Journal**: SAGE Open, January-March 2026, 1–12
- **Methodology**: Quantitative
- **Sample**: 312 valid questionnaires from employees in Chinese digital enterprises (high-tech manufacturing, telecommunications, IT, internet services, new energy)
- **Analysis**: Hierarchical regression and bootstrapping (SPSS 22.0 and Amos 21.0)
- **Data collection**: Stratified random sampling, July 2024

## Research Model

- **Independent variable**: [[digital-leadership]] (6-item scale from Zeike et al., 2019)
- **Dependent variable**: [[innovative-work-behavior]] (6-item scale from Scott & Bruce, 1994, referred to as "Employee Innovative Behavior")
- **Mediator 1**: [[job-crafting]] (15 items selected from Tims et al., 2013)
- **Mediator 2**: [[innovation-efficacy]] (8-item scale from Tierney & Farmer, 2002, referred to as "Employee Innovative Self-Efficacy")
- **Moderator**: [[organizational-innovation-climate]] (10-item scale adapted from Wang et al., 2017)

## Hypotheses and Results

| Hypothesis | Description | Result |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | [[digital-leadership]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovative-work-behavior]] (+) | **Supported** (β = .338, p < .001) |
| H2 | [[digital-leadership]] $\rightarrow$ [[job-crafting]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Mediation) | **Supported** (Point estimate = 0.075, 95% CI [0.014, 0.152]) |
| H3 | [[digital-leadership]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovation-efficacy]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Mediation) | **Supported** (Point estimate = 0.083, 95% CI [0.033, 0.154]) |
| H4 | [[digital-leadership]] $\rightarrow$ [[job-crafting]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovation-efficacy]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Serial Mediation) | **Supported** (Point estimate = 0.067, 95% CI [0.033, 0.124]) |
| H5 | [[organizational-innovation-climate]] moderates [[digital-leadership]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovative-work-behavior]] | **Supported** (β = .333, p < .001); stronger when climate is high. |

## Key Findings

### Direct and Mediated Effects
- Digital leadership significantly predicts employee innovation behavior (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 8).
- Job crafting and innovation efficacy serve as partial mediators, both independently and sequentially (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 9).
- The "black box" of digital leadership influence is unpacked by showing that leaders encourage employees to reshape their work ([[job-crafting]]), which builds confidence in their capabilities ([[innovation-efficacy]]), leading to innovation (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 9).

### Contextual Enhancements
- A supportive organizational innovation climate acts as a catalyst, amplifying the effectiveness of digital leadership in fostering innovation (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 10).
- The relationship between digital leadership and innovative behavior was stronger when the organizational innovation climate was high (simple slope = .54) compared to low (simple slope = .21) (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 9).

## Practical Implications
- **Cultivate Digital Leadership**: Programs should focus on empowerment and psychological support rather than just technical proficiency (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 10).
- **Champion Job Crafting**: Organizations should move away from rigid job descriptions to allow fluid environments for proactive role shaping (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 10).
- **Build Efficacy**: Implement skill-building training combined with opportunities to apply skills in manageably challenging projects (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 10).
- **Prioritize Innovation Climate**: Senior management must commit resources and foster psychological safety for experimentation (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 10).

## Limitation
- **Geographical and Industrial Focus**: The study was limited to digital enterprises in the Haixi Western District of China, which may constrain generalizability (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 10).
- **Cross-Sectional Data**: The use of cross-sectional data precludes definitive causal inferences (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 10).
- **Dimension Specificity**: The study treated job crafting and innovation efficacy as overarching variables; future research should explore their specific dimensions (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 10).
