# Cognitive-Affective Processing System

**Summary**: A psychological framework explaining that individual behavior originates from the interaction between external situational factors and internal cognitive-emotional reactions.

**Sources**: Gao and Gao - 2024.md

---

The Cognitive-Affective Processing System (CAPS) framework posits that a person operates as a complex network where situational cues trigger a cascade of internal cognitive and affective (emotional) responses, which then dictate the individual's ultimate behavior (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 2). 

This theory helps elucidate organizational behavior by showing that focusing purely on cognition or purely on affect is insufficient. For instance, the external situational cue of [[digital-leadership]] acts as a stimulus. This stimulus simultaneously activates a cognitive mechanism (enhancing [[psychological-empowerment]]) and an affective mechanism (influencing [[affective-commitment]]). These internal states interact to uniquely manifest as [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 21).
