# Afif and Fitrianingrum (2025)

**Summary**: An empirical study analyzing the effects of digital leadership, work engagement, and organizational experience on innovative work behavior among Generation Z workers in Central Java.

**Sources**: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md

---

This study focuses on Generation Z workers with organizational experience in Central Java. It uses Partial Least Squares–Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM) on a sample of 271 respondents.

The findings indicate that [[digital-leadership]], [[work-engagement]], and [[organization-experience]] all have a positive and significant impact on [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3108). Among these, [[organization-experience]] showed the most dominant effect (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3119). 

The research is theoretically grounded in [[job-demands-resources-theory]], [[conservation-of-resources-theory]], and the [[communication-as-constitutive-of-organization]] perspective (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3109-3110).
