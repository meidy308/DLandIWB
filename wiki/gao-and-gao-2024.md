# Gao and Gao (2024)

**Summary**: A study investigating how digital leadership fosters employee innovative behavior through psychological empowerment, exploring the cognitive–affective processing system framework.

**Sources**: Gao and Gao - 2024.md

---

This research explores the underlying mechanisms connecting [[digital-leadership]] and [[innovative-work-behavior]] using the [[cognitive-affective-processing-system]] framework. The authors sampled 359 employees to evaluate a dual-path mediation model (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 1).

The findings confirm that digital leadership directly impacts employee innovative behavior (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 5). Importantly, it validates the cognitive path: [[psychological-empowerment]] partially mediates this relationship (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 20). 

In contrast, the affective path via [[affective-commitment]] was positively associated but not statistically significant in this sample (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 20). Furthermore, [[proactive-personality]] did not significantly moderate these relationships, potentially because proactive employees rely less heavily on digital leadership to drive their innovative outcomes than non-proactive employees do (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 21).
