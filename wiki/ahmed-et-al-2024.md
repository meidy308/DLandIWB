# Ahmed et al. (2024)

**Summary**: A two-wave longitudinal study examining the impact of digital leadership on innovative work behavior through learning orientation, innovation capabilities, and the moderating role of leader-member exchange (LMX).

**Sources**: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md

---

This study focuses on 440 employees and managers in the automobile industry in France, investigating the path from perceived [[digital-leadership]] to [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3607).

The findings show that digital leadership significantly impacts [[learning-orientation]], which in turn develops [[innovation-capabilities]] to finally result in [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3607). In addition, the quality of [[leader-member-exchange]] (LMX) acts as a moderator: stronger exchange relationships amplify the positive influence of digital leadership on learning orientation (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3620).

The theoretical grounding of the study is the [[social-exchange-theory]], highlighting how positive managerial relationships trigger reciprocal beneficial behaviors from employees (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3610).
