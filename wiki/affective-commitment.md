# Affective Commitment

**Summary**: An affective attachment to an organization characterized by shared values, a desire to remain, and a willingness to exert effort on its behalf.

**Sources**: Gao and Gao - 2024.md

---

Affective commitment requires an individual to be integrated into interpersonal relationships to maintain and develop them (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 6). 

Within the [[cognitive-affective-processing-system]] framework, this represents the affective (emotional) pathway. Theoretically, when [[digital-leadership]] cultivates trust, safe communication, and employee well-being, employees develop a stronger affective commitment, leading to increased willingness to take risks and demonstrate [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 6).

However, empirical evidence in some studies exploring the link from digital leadership to innovative behavior has found this mediating path to be positive but not statistically significant, suggesting that cognitive routes like [[psychological-empowerment]] might play a stronger primary role (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 20).
