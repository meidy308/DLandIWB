# Dedication

**Summary**: A positive attitude towards one's job characterized by a sense of significance, enthusiasm, inspiration, pride, and challenge.

**Sources**: Bakker et al. - 2003.md

---

Dedication is a core aspect of work engagement and refers to a strong psychological involvement in one's work. While organizational commitment refers to positive attitudes towards the organization as a whole, dedication relates more directly to the positive attitudes towards the job itself (source: Bakker et al. - 2003.md, p. 397).

## Relationship with Other Variables

In the context of the [[job-demands-resources-theory]]:
- **[[job-resources]]**: The motivational process of the JD-R model posits that the presence of job resources (such as social support, supervisory coaching, performance feedback, and time control) fosters dedication. Resources stimulate personal growth and help accomplish work goals, naturally leading to greater enthusiasm and pride (source: Bakker et al. - 2003.md, p. 397-398).
- **[[turnover-intentions]]**: High levels of dedication (along with [[affective-commitment]]) serve as a full mediator between job resources and the intention to quit. Employees who feel a sense of significance and enthusiasm at work are far less inclined to look for another job (source: Bakker et al. - 2003.md, p. 398, 409).

## Measurement

Dedication is often measured as a subscale of the Utrecht Work Engagement Scale (UWES) (Schaufeli et al., 2002). It typically includes five items such as "I am enthusiastic about my job," measured on a frequency scale (e.g., 0 = never, 6 = always) (source: Bakker et al. - 2003.md, p. 401).
