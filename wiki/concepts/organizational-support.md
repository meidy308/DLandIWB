# Organizational Support

**Summary**: The degree to which employees believe their organization values their contributions and cares about their well-being.

**Sources**: Hadi et al. - 2023.md

---

Organizational support (often operationalized as Perceived Organizational Support) includes organizational concern for employees in the form of program design, training, or competency development to increase perceptions of support (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 75). When employees feel the organization cares, provides non-judgmental feedback, and supports their actions, they become more creative and likely to engage in innovative behaviors (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 75).

## Dimensions

Hadi et al. (2024), citing Umihastanti & Frianto (2022), operationalize organizational support through four dimensions:
1. **Awards**: Valuing and appreciating extra effort.
2. **Development**: Training opportunities, further studies, and certification.
3. **Working conditions**: Vertical-horizontal interactions and comfortable infrastructure.
4. **Welfare**: Intrinsic and extrinsic support for well-being (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 77).

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **As an Independent Variable (IV)**:
    - **Innovative Work Behavior**: Found to have a positive and significant effect on IWB ($B = 0.299$ direct, $B = 0.587$ total, $p = 0.006$; source: [[hadi-et-al-2024]]).
    - **Emotional Intelligence**: Found to have a significant positive impact on emotional intelligence ($B = 0.288$, $p = 0.000$; source: [[hadi-et-al-2024]]).

## Measurement

In the study by [[hadi-et-al-2024]], organizational support was measured using 12 statements with high reliability ($\alpha = 0.956$; source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 78).

## Contradictions

Findings on organizational support and IWB have been inconsistent. While [[hadi-et-al-2024]] and Dogru (2018) found significant positive effects, some earlier studies by the same authors (Hadi et al., 2020) indicated that organizational support might not always have a significant positive effect on IWB (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 75).
