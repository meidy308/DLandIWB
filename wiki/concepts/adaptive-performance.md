# Adaptive Performance

**Summary**: The ability of employees to adjust their behavior and performance in response to changing work demands, environmental conditions, and organizational requirements.

**Sources**: Deviren - 2026.md

---

Adaptive performance refers to the capacity of individuals and organizations to maintain effectiveness while adjusting to evolving environmental conditions. It is a critical indicator of organizational sustainability in dynamic industries (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 32).

## Dimensions

Adaptive performance consists of eight dimensions (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 33):

1. Adaptation to stressful situations
2. Adaptation to uncertain situations
3. Adaptation to physical situations
4. Adaptation to cultural situations
5. Adaptation to technological situations
6. Interpersonal adaptability
7. Problem-solving and innovative thinking
8. Learning

## Relationship with Digital Leadership and IWB

In the automotive sector, [[digital-leadership]], [[digital-organizational-culture]], and [[innovative-work-behavior]] together explain **87%** of the variance in adaptive performance (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 40).

- Digital leadership has a strong positive direct effect on adaptive performance (B = 0.60, p < .01) (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 40).
- Digital culture also has a strong positive direct effect (B = 0.79, p < .01) (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 40).
- IWB has a significant but smaller positive direct effect (B = 0.19, p < .01) (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 40).

A strong relationship exists between adaptive performance and [[innovative-work-behavior]]. IWB forms the basis of adaptive performance, playing a central role in generating and implementing new ideas (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 32).

## Moderation and Mediation Effects

- [[digital-organizational-culture]] **negatively moderates** the DL → AP relationship: when digital culture is strong, it absorbs some of the influence that digital leadership would otherwise exert on adaptive performance (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 42–43).
- [[innovative-work-behavior]] has a **negative mediating effect** on DL → AP: the indirect path through IWB slightly reduces the total effect of digital leadership on adaptive performance (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 43).

## Measurement

The study used the Adaptive Performance Scale developed by Charbonnier-Voirin & Roussel (2012), adapted into Turkish by Üstün & Büyükbaş (2020). It is a unidimensional 5-point Likert scale comprising 19 items (source: Deviren - 2026.md, p. 36).
