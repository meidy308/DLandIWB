# Affective Commitment to Leadership

**Summary**: An individual's emotional attachment, identification with, and trust in their leader, which drives reciprocal efforts such as innovation.

**Sources**: Li and Xiao - 2023.md

---

Affective commitment to leadership (or supervisor affective commitment) represents the psychological recognition and identification of an employee with their manager. Unlike organizational commitment, which relates to the whole company, this variable focuses on the interpersonal bond between the subordinate and the leader (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 6). It is derived from the psychological recognition of values and identification with interactions.

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As Mediator
- **[[digital-leadership]] → Affective Commitment → [[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Digital leadership significantly predicts affective commitment to the leader ($B = 0.251, p < .01$). From a "Relationship" perspective, leaders who use digital technology to provide resources and solve work challenges increase team members' recognition of worth and trust. According to [[social-exchange-theory]] and the reciprocity principle, employees feel motivated to "repay" this support by engaging in extra-role behaviors, primarily innovation (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 7, 11).

### Moderation by Culture
- **[[power-distance]]** negatively moderates the link between digital leadership and affective commitment ($B = -0.154, p < .01$). Employees with high power distance are less sensitive to the leader's digital caring behaviors and believe more in formal authority than emotional connection, reducing the leadership's impact on their emotional attachment (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 13).

## Measurement

Measured using the Supervisor Affective Commitment Scale (developed by Weng Jie, 2018), consisting of 5 items on a 5-point Likert scale (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 11). Example items include: "As a subordinate, I am delighted to take work from my team supervisor" and "I honestly believe that the team supervisor's affairs are my own affairs."
