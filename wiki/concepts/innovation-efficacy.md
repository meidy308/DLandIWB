# Innovation Efficacy

**Summary**: An employee's belief in their capability to produce and execute innovative outcomes at work.

**Sources**: Huang et al. - 2026.md

---

Innovation efficacy (also referred to as **Innovative Self-Efficacy** or **Creative Self-Efficacy**) is a critical driver of intrinsic motivation. It represents the psychological state where an individual feels confident in their ability to handle novel situations and solve complex problems (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 3).

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As a Mediator
- **Digital Leadership and IWB**: Innovation efficacy mediates the relationship between [[digital-leadership]] and [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Point estimate = 0.083, 95% CI [0.033, 0.154]) (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 8).
- **Sequential Mediation Chain**: It serves as the second mediator in the chain: [[digital-leadership]] $\rightarrow$ [[job-crafting]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovation-efficacy]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Point estimate = 0.067, 95% CI [0.033, 0.124]) (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 8).

### As an Antecedent
- **Innovative Behavior**: Higher innovation efficacy leads to increased engagement in innovative activities as employees view challenges as opportunities (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 4).

### As a Consequent
- **Job Crafting**: The process of job crafting provides mastery experiences that reinforcement the belief in one's ability to handle novel tasks, thus enhancing innovation efficacy (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 4).
- **Digital Leadership**: Digital leadership fosters efficacy by promoting the acquisition of new digital skills and providing necessary resources (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 3).

## Measurement

- **Tierney and Farmer (2002)**: An 8-item scale. Sample items include: "I am capable of creatively achieving most of the goals I set for myself" and "In general, I believe I can accomplish significant creative outcomes" (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 5).
