# Social Role Theory

**Summary**: A grand theory positing that behavioral and psychological differences between men and women are largely influenced by socially constructed gender roles.

**Sources**: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md

---

Social role theory helps explain how societal expectations surrounding gender shape an individual's reaction to different work environments and leadership styles (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 44). Within the workplace, expectations tied to gender roles can alter the dynamics of creativity, motivation, and leadership reception.

For instance, when evaluating the impact of [[digital-leadership]], social role theory suggests that male and female employees may utilize different mechanisms to express motivation or engage in problem-solving. Research indicates that the influence of digital leadership on [[intrinsic-motivation]] and [[problem-solving-creativity]] is significantly more pronounced among female employees compared to their male counterparts (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 52).
