# Organization Experience

**Summary**: The accumulated knowledge, skills, and social interactions gained from active participation in organizational activities.

**Sources**: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md

---

Organization experience encompasses the practical knowledge and interpersonal competencies individuals acquire through active involvement in formal and informal organizations. It shapes adaptive capabilities, communication patterns, and meaning-making, as explained by the [[communication-as-constitutive-of-organization]] perspective (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3109–3110).

## Dimensions

Organization experience consists of three dimensions (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3114–3115):

1. **Framing** — Practical application of organizational knowledge in the work environment.
2. **Co-creating** — Involving team members in collaborative work processes.
3. **Connecting** — Establishing effective communication and relationships among members.

## Relationship with Other Variables

Organization experience has the **strongest positive effect** on [[innovative-work-behavior]] among the three independent variables tested (β = 0.412, t = 5.759, p = 0.000, F² = 0.170, moderate effect). Individuals with rich organizational experience develop broader creative problem-solving methods, courage in decision-making, and collaboration skills that formal training or technology cannot easily replace (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3119–3120).

It is also theoretically positioned as a job resource under [[conservation-of-resources-theory]], where the accumulation of experience-based resources produces positive workplace behaviors (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3109).

## Measurement

Measured using a 5-point Likert scale with three indicators: framing, co-creating, and connecting. Items adapted from prior literature (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3114–3115). Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.832, Composite Reliability = 0.899 (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3117).
