# Psychological Capital

**Summary**: A positive mental state characterized by self-efficacy, optimism, hope, and resilience that serves as a vital psychological resource for individual performance and innovation.

**Sources**: Li and Xiao - 2023.md

---

Psychological Capital (PsyCap) is defined as an individual’s positive psychological state of development, encompassing four core components: self-efficacy, optimism, hope, and resilience (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 4). While traditionally distinct from human and social capital, PsyCap has been identified as a more pervasive predictor of employee behavior in modern organizational management.

## Dimensions

Psychological capital consists of four distinct but interrelated dimensions (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 4):
1. **Self-efficacy**: The confidence to take on and put in the necessary effort to succeed at challenging tasks.
2. **Optimism**: Making a positive attribution about succeeding now and in the future.
3. **Hope**: Persevering toward goals and, when necessary, redirecting paths to goals in order to succeed.
4. **Resilience**: When beset by problems and adversity, sustaining and bouncing back and even beyond to attain success.

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As Mediator
- **[[digital-leadership]] → PsyCap → [[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Digital leadership significantly predicts PsyCap ($B = 0.356, p < .01$). From a "Self" perspective, leaders who utilize digital media effectively communicate care and support, which boosts employees' internal stores of self-efficacy and resilience. This internal psychological strength then provides the motivation and resilience required to explore and implement innovative ideas (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 11, 16).

### Moderation by Culture
- **[[power-distance]]** negatively moderates the link between digital leadership and PsyCap ($B = -0.116, p < .05$). In high power distance environments, employees are less sensitive to the "supportive" signals sent by leaders through digital channels, dampening the positive influence of leadership on their psychological state (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 13).

## Measurement

Li and Xiao (2023) measured PsyCap using the Psychological Capital Scale (based on Hu Chunfang / Wen et al., 2009) with 16 items across 4 dimensions on a 5-point Likert scale (source: Li and Xiao - 2023.md, p. 11).
