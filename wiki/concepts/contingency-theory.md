# Contingency Theory

**Summary**: A management and leadership theory suggesting that there is no single best way to lead an organization; rather, the optimal leadership style is contingent upon the internal and external situation and the leader's understanding of these circumstances.

**Sources**: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md

---

Contingency theory posits that a leader's ability to lead depends on how well they comprehend the circumstances. The core idea is that every organization is unique and deals with different issues, requiring different leadership actions for different situations (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 85-86).

## Key Principles

- **Situational Leadership**: Leadership is a situation-dependent state that requires different demands and applications depending on time and place (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 86).
- **No "One Size Fits All"**: Leadership style must be linked to contingency factors (various situations). A style that works in one context may not work in another (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 86).

## Application in Research

In the study of [[digital-leadership]], contingency theory is used to explain why digital leaders must adapt their style to foster organizational change and motivation based on the specific technological and cultural context of the organization (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 85).
