# Hindrance Demands

**Summary**: Stressful job demands that employees tend to appraise as constraints, barriers, or roadblocks that thwart personal growth, learning, and goal attainment.

**Sources**: Crawford et al. - 2010.md

---

Hindrance demands are those aspects of the job that interfere with an individual's ability to do their work effectively and provide no potential for personal gain. Based on the [[transactional-theory-of-stress]], these demands trigger negative emotions (e.g., fear, anxiety, anger) and passive, emotion-focused coping styles (e.g., withdrawal) (source: Crawford et al. - 2010.md, p. 837).

## Dimensions

Key examples of hindrance demands include:
- **Role Conflict**: Conflicting demands from different sources (ρ = -0.24 with engagement).
- **Role Ambiguity**: Lack of clarity regarding job expectations and responsibilities.
- **Organizational Politics**: Perceived favoritism and self-serving behaviors in the workplace (ρ = -0.25 with engagement).
- **Red Tape / Administrative Hassles**: Unnecessary bureaucratic hurdles (ρ = -0.17 with engagement).
- **Resource Inadequacies**: Lacking the information or equipment needed to perform tasks (ρ = -0.18 with engagement).
- **Role Overload**: Having more tasks than can be handled within available time/resources (ρ = -0.20 with engagement).

(source: Crawford et al. - 2010.md, p. 836, 842)

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Negative Predictor of [[work-engagement]]**: Hindrance demands actively detach employees from their work roles (ρ = -0.19, β = -0.19) (source: Crawford et al. - 2010.md, p. 840).
- **Positive Predictor of [[burnout]]**: They consume significant mental resources and lead to severe energy depletion (ρ = 0.30, β = 0.25) (source: Crawford et al. - 2010.md, p. 840).
- **Relationship with [[job-resources]]**: Hindrance demands are particularly damaging when resources are low, as employees lack the means to overcome the obstacles (source: Crawford et al. - 2010.md, p. 837).

## Measurement

Hindrance demands are typically measured using established scales for role conflict, role ambiguity, and organizational politics (source: Crawford et al. - 2010.md, p. 839).

## Contradictions

While [[challenge-demands]] can have a "paradoxical" positive effect on engagement, hindrance demands are consistently negative across all outcomes (source: Crawford et al. - 2010.md, p. 842).
