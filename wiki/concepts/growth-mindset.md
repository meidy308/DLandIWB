# Growth Mindset

**Summary**: The belief that one's abilities, intelligence, and competencies can be developed and enhanced through effort, learning, and persistence.

**Sources**: [[Soediro et al. - 2025.md]]

---

Growth mindset reflects an individual's openness to growth and their adaptation to challenges. It fosters resilience and adaptability, which are key traits for innovative work behavior (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 116, 132). Individuals with a growth mindset tend to view challenges as opportunities for growth rather than insurmountable obstacles (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 255).

## Dimensions

While often treated as a unidimensional construct in psychological studies (as per Dweck, 2009), its manifestation in workplace innovation includes (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 132):
- **Resilience**: Ability to recover from setbacks during the creative process.
- **Adaptability**: Openness to changing work patterns, especially technology-based ones.
- **Propensity to embrace challenges**: Proactive engagement with novel problems.

## Relationship with Other Variables

Growth mindset serves as an important psychological antecedent for innovation, particularly in environments undergoing digital transformation (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 130).

- **Predictor of [[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Growth mindset positively influences IWB (β = 0.172, p = 0.001), as it encourages continuous learning and creative problem-solving (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 347).
- **Predictor of [[creative-process-engagement]]**: Growth mindset has a strong significant influence on creative process engagement (β = 0.434, p = 0.000), meaning it plays a crucial role in how individuals immerse themselves in ideation (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 255).
- **Indirect effect via [[creative-process-engagement]]**: The relationship between growth mindset and IWB is partially mediated by CPE (31.84% mediation) (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 336).

## Measurement

Typically measured using scales developed by Carol Dweck or adapted versions for the work context. Soediro et al. (2025) measured growth mindset using a 5-point Likert scale (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 229).
