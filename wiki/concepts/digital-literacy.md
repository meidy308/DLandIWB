# Digital Literacy

**Summary**: The individual awareness, attitudes, and capabilities that promote the effective use of digital tools to access, manage, integrate, evaluate, and synthesize digital resources.

**Sources**: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md

---

Digital literacy is the capacity to effectively and critically access, evaluate, and create information using a range of digital technologies (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 5). It enables workers to comprehend and innovatively utilize digital technologies in problem solving, thereby augmenting their contributions to innovation processes (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 5).

## Dimensions

The digital skills framework for the workforce comprises seven competencies (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 5):
1.  **Operating software and devices**
2.  **Data and information literacy**
3.  **Communication and collaboration**
4.  **Creating digital content**
5.  **Safety**
6.  **Problem solving**
7.  **Career-related competencies**

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As a Mediator
- **[[digital-leadership]]** → **Digital Literacy** → **[[employee-performance]]**: Digital literacy partially mediates the connection between digital leadership and the work performance of sales employees (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 16).
- **[[digital-leadership]]** → **Digital Literacy** → **[[innovative-work-behavior]]** → **[[employee-performance]]**: Digital literacy acts as a dual mediator in a sequential chain, improving productivity and elucidating how creative work behaviors translate into job outcomes (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 16).

### As a Moderator
- **Strengthens DL → IWB**: High digital literacy enhances the promotional impact of digital leadership on innovative behavior ($\beta = 0.085, p = .000$) (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 14, 16).
- **Strengthens DL → Performance**: Digital literacy boosts the link between digital leadership and actual work performance ($\beta = 0.081, p = .010$) (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 14).
- **Non-significant in IWB → Performance**: In specific retail contexts, digital literacy may not significantly moderate the path between innovation behavior and final performance results ($\beta = -0.042, p = .180$) (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 14).

## Measurement

Digital literacy can be measured using scales adapted from Law et al. (2018) and Nikou et al. (2022). Items typically include confidence in technical problem solving, learning speed for new technologies, and ability to search for/evaluate digital information. Reliability testing shows high consistency ($\alpha = 0.878$, AVE = 0.732) (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 10).
