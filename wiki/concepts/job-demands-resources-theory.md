# Job Demands-Resources Theory

**Summary**: A theoretical framework explaining how job resources and job demands interact to affect employee well-being and performance.

**Sources**: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, Zia et al. - 2024.md

---

In the context of innovation, Job Demands-Resources (JD-R) Theory posits that the availability of job resources, such as leadership and organizational experience, can elevate employee engagement and subsequently foster [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3109-3110). 

Zia et al. (2024) specifically apply JD-R to the digital domain, conceptualizing [[digital-communication]] and [[digital-training]] as job resources that help employees cope with digital demands and achieve goals through increased [[digital-engagement]] (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 5).
