# Learning Orientation

**Summary**: An active dedication to continuously enhancing skills, understanding, capabilities, and expertise, both at the individual and organizational levels.

**Sources**: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md

---

Learning orientation is a pivotal internal motivator that drives individuals to actively seek challenges that facilitate learning and personal growth. At the organizational level, it reflects values emphasizing the creation, sharing, and application of essential knowledge (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3612, 3615).

## Dimensions

Learning orientation encompasses several interrelated values (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3614–3615):

1. **Commitment to learning** — Continuous enhancement of skills and knowledge.
2. **Open-mindedness** — Willingness to question existing assumptions and adopt new approaches.
3. **Shared vision** — Organizational alignment toward learning goals.

## Relationship with Other Variables

Learning orientation serves as a **critical mediator** in the DL → IWB pathway:

- [[digital-leadership]] significantly predicts learning orientation (β = 0.548, t = 10.867, p = 0.000), explaining 41% of its variance (R² = 0.410). This relationship is strengthened by [[leader-member-exchange]] (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3619–3620).
- Learning orientation in turn predicts [[innovation-capabilities]] (β = 0.453, t = 7.586, p = 0.000), explaining 20.5% of its variance (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3619).
- The mediation from DL → LO → IC is significant (β = 0.248, t = 5.016, p = 0.000) (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3622).

Learning-oriented employees accumulate valuable expertise over time, making them more capable of independently generating innovative solutions (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3615–3616).

## Measurement

Measured using a 5-item, 7-point Likert scale adapted from Aboobaker & Zakkariya (2020). Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.826, Composite Reliability = 0.878, AVE = 0.592 (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3617–3618).
