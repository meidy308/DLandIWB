# Psychological Ownership

**Summary**: A cognitive and emotional state where individuals perceive a sense of possession and deep attachment to their work or organization.

**Sources**: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, Gao and Gao - 2024.md

---

Psychological ownership refers to a state in which individuals feel as though the target of ownership (often the organization or specific tasks) is "theirs." It is distinct from legal ownership and fosters a stronger sense of responsibility, commitment, and identification with organizational goals (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 2). It is often considered a core motivational construct linking leadership behavior with employee-level innovation (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).

## Dimensions

According to Sebetci et al. (2025), psychological ownership is multi-dimensional (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 4):
- **Emotional Ownership**: The affective and emotional connection an individual feels toward the organization or work.
- **Work-Based Ownership**: The perception of possession and responsibility focused specifically on task-level activities and job-related domains.

## Relationship with Other Variables

### Antecedents
- **[[digital-leadership]]**: Digital leaders, particularly those with supportive and innovative characteristics, cultivate ownership by emphasizing employee learning and empowerment (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 2; Ahmed et al. - 2024.md).
- **[[psychological-empowerment]]**: Leadership styles grounded in empowerment create conditions conducive to ownership attitudes (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 2; Gao and Gao - 2024.md).

### Outcomes
- **[[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Psychological ownership serves as a key driver for employees to identify opportunities and implement new ideas. Work-based ownership specifically shows a stronger mediating effect in tech environments (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).
- **[[knowledge-sharing-behavior]]**: Ownership cultivates a sense of responsibility and accountability toward knowledge-based tasks, facilitating collaborative practices (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).
- **Motivation and Loyalty**: Ownership leads to enhanced motivation, job satisfaction, and loyalty (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 2).

## Measurement

Across studies, measuring psychological ownership often uses:
- **Shukla and Singh (2015)**: Multi-dimensional scale measuring Emotional and Work-Based ownership (5 items each; 5-point Likert) (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 4).

## Contradictions

In technology-focused environments, **Work-Based Ownership** consistently explains higher variance in outcomes (like [[innovative-work-behavior]]) compared to **Emotional Ownership**, suggesting task-level attachment is more critical than affective connection alone in these sectors (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).
