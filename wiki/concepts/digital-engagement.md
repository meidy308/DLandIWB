# Digital Engagement

**Summary**: A positive, work-related psychological state characterized by the intensity and direction of energy expended on digital platforms and technology.

**Sources**: Zia et al. - 2024.md

---

Digital engagement is defined as the "active, work-related positive psychological state operationalized by the intensity and direction of cognitive, emotional, and behavioral energy expended on digital platforms and using technology" (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 2, citing Jesuthasan, 2017). It represents the effort and time employees devote to digital platforms and systems within an organization.

## Dimensions

Digital engagement is characterized by three types of energy:
- **Cognitive energy**: Intellectual focus and attention on digital tasks.
- **Emotional energy**: Positive affect and enthusiasm towards using digital tools.
- **Behavioral energy**: The physical effort and persistence in using technology for work.

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Antecedents**:
    - [[digital-communication]] positively predicts digital engagement ($\beta=0.147, p=0.009$) (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 11).
    - [[digital-training]] was found to be a non-significant predictor among technological professionals in one study ($\beta=0.059, p=0.141$) (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 11).
- **Outcomes**:
    - Digital engagement positively influences [[digital-leadership]] capabilities ($\beta=0.653, p<0.001$) (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 11).
- **Mediator Role**:
    - Acts as a serial mediator (with [[digital-leadership]]) between digital job resources and [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 12).

## Measurement

- **Adapted Engagement Scale**: Zia et al. (2024) adapted a 6-item scale from Shuck et al. (2017), appending the term "digital" to items (e.g., "While performing the digital job task, I am really focused").
