# Digital Competency

**Summary**: An individual’s knowledge, skills, and attitudes in accessing, utilizing, creating, and sharing digital resources efficiently and collaboratively.

**Sources**: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md

---

Digital competency encompasses the ability to communicate and collaborate through digital technologies to achieve specific objectives (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 476). It serves as a fundamental foundation for innovation by equipping employees with the technical and collaborative capacities required to realize value-creating ideas (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483).

## Dimensions

Key dimensions or indicators of digital competency include:
- Accessing digital resources
- Efficient utilization of digital tools
- Digital resource creation
- Collaborative use of digital technologies (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 476)
- Problem-solving and cross-functional collaboration (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483)

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As a Mediator
- **[[digital-talent-development]]** → **Digital Competency** → **[[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Digital competency mediates the effect of talent development on innovation ($B = 0.094, p = 0.036$). Higher levels of digital competence are positively associated with a greater likelihood of demonstrating innovative behaviors, such as seeking new solutions and applying technology creatively (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 482).

### Relationship with Performance
- Digital competence contributes to organizational performance by enabling employees to transform creative ideas into actionable innovations (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483).

## Measurement

In the study by Fauziah et al. (2026), digital competency was measured using 5 indicators. Reliability testing showed a Cronbach's Alpha of 0.896 and an AVE of 0.546 (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 480).
