# Person-Job Fit

**Summary**: The degree of compatibility between an individual and the specific tasks, goals, and requirements of their job.

**Sources**: Wang et al. - 2025.md

---

Person-job (P-J) fit is a core dimension of [[person-environment-fit-theory]]. It reflects how well an employee's knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSA) match the job demands, and how well the job supplies meet the employee's needs and preferences (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 2).

## Dimensions

P-J fit is traditionally divided into two categories (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 5):
- **Demands–Abilities (D–A) Fit**: The match between environmental (job) demands and personal abilities.
- **Needs–Supplies (N–S) Fit**: The match between the employee's needs and the organization's ability to provide rewards or resources that meet those needs.

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As a Moderator
- **Job Crafting and Innovation**: P-J fit positively moderates the relationship between **cognitive crafting** and [[innovative-work-behavior]]. High P-J fit ensures that the new meanings employees assign to their work are compatible with organizational needs, leading to higher performance (B = 0.111, p < .05) (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).
- **Moderated Mediation**: It moderates the indirect effect of [[digital-leadership]] on innovation performance through cognitive crafting. The mediating effect is significantly stronger when P-J fit is high (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).

### As an Outcome
- **General Outcomes**: High P-J fit is associated with increased job satisfaction, organizational commitment, and reduced turnover intentions (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 2).

## Measurement

- **Cable and DeRue (2002)**: A 6-item scale measuring subjective perceptions of fit. Items include "There is a good fit between what my job offers me and what I am looking for in a job" (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 7).
