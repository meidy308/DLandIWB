# Psychological Empowerment

**Summary**: A set of motivational cognitions including meaning, competence, self-determination, and impact that reflect an employee's orientation toward their job role.

**Sources**: Gao and Gao - 2024.md

---

Psychological empowerment consists of four dimensions:
- *Meaning*: the value of individual goal achievement according to predetermined standards.
- *Competence*: equivalent to self-efficacy.
- *Self-determination*: possessing autonomy and the power to initiate and regulate work behavior.
- *Impact*: the degree to which an individual believes they can influence work outcomes.
(source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 5)

In the context of the [[cognitive-affective-processing-system]], psychological empowerment represents the cognitive pathway through which external situational factors manifest as behavior. Specifically, [[digital-leadership]] provides an environment of transparency, capability building, and reduced resistance to change, functioning to significantly elevate an employee's psychological empowerment (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 5).

With higher psychological empowerment, employees are more likely to exhibit [[innovative-work-behavior]]. Feelings of autonomy and self-determination give employees a safe space to try new ideas and accept trial-and-error processes without fear of punishment (source: Gao and Gao - 2024.md, p. 5).
