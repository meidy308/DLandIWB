# Health Impairment Process

**Summary**: One of the two dual pathways in [[job-demands-resources-theory]], describing how high job demands lead to exhaustion and health issues.

**Sources**: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, Demerouti et al. - 2001.md

---

The Health Impairment Process is an energetic pathway within the JD-R framework. It posits that poorly designed jobs or chronic [[job-demands]] (e.g., work overload, emotional demands) exhaust employees' mental and physical resources (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 9).

## Dimensions

- **Energy Depletion**: Sustained effort leads to the consumption of energetic resources.
- **Strain**: Manifests as [[burnout]] (specifically [[exhaustion]]).
- **Health Outcomes**: Prolonged strain leads to psychosomatic complaints, repetitive strain injury (RSI), and long-term sickness absence (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 10).

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Predictor**: Chronic [[job-demands]] (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 9).
- **Mediator**: [[exhaustion]] and burnout often mediate the link between demands and ill-health (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 11).
- **Outcomes**: Negatively impacts [[job-performance]] and increases [[absenteeism]] (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 10).

## Measurement

Typically measured using structural equation modeling (SEM) paths from demands to burnout components and then to health or performance metrics.

## Contradictions

While initially seen as a purely top-down process, the theory now recognizes "loss spirals" where ill-health can lead to the perception or creation of more demands (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 14).
