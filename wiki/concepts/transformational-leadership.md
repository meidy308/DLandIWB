# Transformational Leadership

**Summary**: A leadership style characterized by inspirational motivation, individual consideration, and intellectual stimulation, which fosters employee well-being by creating a resourceful work environment.

**Sources**: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md

---

Transformational leadership involves behaviors that inspire followers to transcend their own self-interests for the good of the organization. In the context of [[job-demands-resources-theory]], it is viewed as an "upstream" factor that indirectly influences employee outcomes by modifying their [[job-demands]] and [[job-resources]] (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 280).

## Dimensions

Transformational leadership typically includes:
- **Inspirational Motivation**: Creating a compelling vision.
- **Individualized Consideration**: Attending to each follower's needs.
- **Intellectual Stimulation**: Encouraging creativity and new approaches.
- **Idealized Influence**: Serving as a role model.

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As an Antecedent to Job Resources
Followers report more job resources—such as autonomy, feedback, and opportunities for growth—when their leader exhibits transformational behaviors (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 280).

### Impact on Work Engagement and Performance
Transformational leadership positively influences [[work-engagement]] and job performance because these leaders create abundant resources for their followers to deal with daily challenges (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 280).

### Moderating Role
In highly demanding situations (e.g., complex exercises or "hurricanes" at sea), transformational leaders provide the daily social support and autonomy needed to maintain engagement (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 280).

## Measurement

The most common measurement tool is the **Multifactor Leadership Questionnaire (MLQ)** by Bass and Avolio, which assesses the four dimensions mentioned above. In JD-R research, diary studies (e.g., Breevaart et al., 2014) are often used to capture daily fluctuations in leadership behavior.

## Contradictions

While transformational leadership is generally positive, some studies suggest its effects may be fully mediated by job characteristics, meaning its "direct" effect on performance might be non-significant once resources are accounted for (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2017.md, p. 280).
