# Person-Environment Fit Theory

**Summary**: A theory suggesting that behavior is a function of the compatibility between an individual and their work environment.

**Sources**: Huang et al. - 2026.md

---

Person-Environment (P-E) Fit Theory (French et al., 1974; Edwards et al., 1998) posits that a high degree of "fit" between an individual's needs and the resources provided by the environment leads to positive outcomes. In the context of digital transformation, [[digital-leadership]] fulfills employees' needs for innovative resources and behavioral stimulation by providing essential digital tools and support (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 2).

## Application in Innovation Research

The theory explains the moderating role of environmental factors like the [[organizational-innovation-climate]]. A supportive climate creates an environment highly congruent with the objectives and means provided by digital leaders, thereby amplifying the leader's effectiveness (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 4).
