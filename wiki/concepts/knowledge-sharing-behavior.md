# Knowledge Sharing Behavior

**Summary**: The process through which employees exchange ideas, expertise, and experiences to support individual and collective learning.

**Sources**: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md

---

Knowledge sharing behavior (KSB) is a critical component of organizational knowledge management systems and a key enabler of innovation and competitive advantage (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 3). It involves the exchange of both tacit and explicit knowledge to reinforce collective problem-solving capacity.

## Dimensions

Knowledge sharing is often multi-dimensional. In the study by Sebetci et al. (2025), focusing on the **Usefulness/Appropriateness** dimension (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 5). Other studies (cited in the source) highlight:
- Tacit knowledge sharing
- Explicit knowledge sharing

## Relationship with Other Variables

### Antecedents
- **[[digital-leadership]]**: Leaders who embrace digital tools and cultivate an innovation-oriented culture can actively support knowledge exchange (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 3).
- **[[psychological-ownership]]**: Serving as a key antecedent, ownership (particularly work-based/task-level) cultivates a deeper sense of responsibility toward knowledge-based tasks (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).
- **Organizational Culture**: Effective sharing relies on leadership behaviors that promote open communication and reduce barriers to collaboration (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 3).

### Outcomes
- **[[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Knowledge sharing is a fundamental precursor to innovation effectiveness in both public and private sectors (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 2).
- **Sustainable Performance**: Digital leadership plays a critical role in embedding knowledge sharing into organizational routines, driving sustainable performance improvements (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 3).

## Measurement

Measurement scales for KSB include:
- **Alakurt and Keser (2016)**: A scale originally including five sub-dimensions; often narrowed to the "Usefulness/Appropriateness" dimension (4 items; 5-point Likert) for specific research (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 5).

## Contradictions

While emotional connection (emotional ownership) may enhance interpersonal trust within teams, task-oriented ownership appears more effective in facilitating professional knowledge-sharing practices in technical environments (source: Sebetci et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).
