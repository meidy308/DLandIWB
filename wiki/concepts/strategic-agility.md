# Strategic Agility

**Summary**: An organization’s capacity to detect early signals of environmental change and respond effectively by rapidly reconfiguring internal resources and capabilities.

**Sources**: Khan et al. - 2025.md

---

Strategic agility captures how well an organization can notice signals of change and respond effectively, often by reconfiguring its internal assets (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 2). It differs from operational agility by focusing on broad shifts in market trends, competitor launches, and regulatory demands (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 5).

## Dimensions

Key capabilities of strategic agility include:
- **Sensing**: The ability to spot opportunities and threats early.
- **Seizing**: The capacity to capture value from sensed opportunities.
- **Reconfiguring**: The continuous transformation and alignment of organizational resources.

## Relationship with Other Variables

In the research by [[khan-et-al-2025]], the following relationships were established:
- **Digital Leadership**: SA acts as a moderator that amplifies the effectiveness of digital leadership. When agility is high, DL has a significantly stronger impact on the [[green-organizational-climate]] (β = 0.36, p < .001).
- **Green Innovative Behavior**: SA moderates the indirect effect of leadership on behavior. In firms with low strategic agility, the link between digital leadership and green innovation becomes **non-significant** (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 11).
- **Amplifier Role**: Agility is conceptualized not just as a reactive trait but as an active force that pushes leadership efforts further, allowing firms to translate climate-driven intentions into actual innovations.

## Measurement

- **Scale**: 8-item scale adapted from Idris and Al Rubaie (2013).
- **Sample items**:
  - "Our company responds to changes in aggregate consumer demand."
  - "Our company reacts to new product or service launches by competitors."
  - "Our company adopts new technologies to produce better, faster and cheaper products and services."
  - "Our company change (expand or reduce) the variety of products/services available for sale."
- **Likert**: 5-point scale (1: Strongly Disagree, 5: Strongly Agree).
