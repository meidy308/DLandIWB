# Leader-Member Exchange

**Summary**: The quality of the localized, two-way relationship between leaders and their individual subordinates, characterized by varying degrees of trust, affection, and mutual respect.

**Sources**: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md

---

Leader-Member Exchange (LMX) theory posits that leaders develop different relationships with different employees depending on mutual expectations, trust, and fulfillment over time. Some interactions are marked by high trust, affection, and mutual respect, while others are limited to the boundaries of the employment contract (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3608–3609).

## Dimensions

**Ahmed et al. (2024)** — High-quality interactions are characterized by mutual trust, affection, respect, and frequent communication (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3613).

**Gehan and Yulianti (2024)** — Measures LMX through 4 dimensions (based on Liden & Maslyn, 1998) (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 34):
1. **Affect** — Mutual liking based on interpersonal attraction.
2. **Loyalty** — Expressions of public support for each other.
3. **Contribution** — Perception of the amount/quality of work-oriented activity each member puts toward mutual goals.
4. **Professional Respect** — Perception of the degree to which each member has built a reputation within or outside the organization.

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As Moderator
- **Moderating DL → Learning Orientation**: LMX quality significantly amplifies the positive effect of digital leadership on learning orientation (β = 0.044, t = 3.069, p = 0.002) (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3620).
- **Non-significant moderation of IWB → Performance**: Among millennials in East Java, LMX was found **not** to significantly moderate the relationship between innovative work behavior and employee performance ($p = .610$) (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 37). This suggests that for this demographic, the translation of innovation into performance results may be more influenced by other factors (e.g., resources, motivation) than the relationship with the leader.

Within high-quality LMX environments:
- Employees gain more autonomy and decision-making authority
- They receive immediate feedback on innovative ideas
- They secure necessary resources and support for implementation
- They feel more confident to voice opinions without fear of repercussions
(source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3613)

LMX is grounded in [[social-exchange-theory]]: high-quality exchanges are perceived as incentives from managers, compelling employees to reciprocate with positive behaviors (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3610–3611).

## Measurement

- **Ahmed et al. (2024)**: 5-item, 7-point Likert scale. Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.760, CR = 0.830, AVE = 0.507 (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3617–3618).
- **Gehan and Yulianti (2024)**: 9 indicators (after validation), 5-point Likert. Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.880, CR = 0.905, AVE = 0.523 (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 35-36).
