# Leader-Member Exchange

**Summary**: The quality of the localized, two-way relationship between leaders and their individual subordinates, characterized by varying degrees of trust, affection, and mutual respect.

**Sources**: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md

---

Leader-Member Exchange (LMX) theory posits that leaders develop different relationships with different employees depending on mutual expectations, trust, and fulfillment over time. Some interactions are marked by high trust, affection, and mutual respect, while others are limited to the boundaries of the employment contract (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3608–3609).

## Dimensions

High-quality LMX interactions are characterized by (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3613):

1. **Mutual trust** — Confidence in each other's intentions and competence.
2. **Affection** — Positive emotional regard between leader and member.
3. **Respect** — Recognition of each other's professional standing.
4. **Frequent communication** — Open exchange of ideas and feedback.

## Relationship with Other Variables

LMX acts as a **moderator** between [[digital-leadership]] and [[learning-orientation]]. When LMX quality is high, the positive effect of perceived digital leadership on an employee's learning orientation is significantly amplified (β = 0.044, t = 3.069, p = 0.002). The relationship is significant at low, medium, and high levels of LMX, but strengthens with quality (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3620–3621).

Within high-quality LMX environments:
- Employees gain more autonomy and decision-making authority
- They receive immediate feedback on innovative ideas
- They secure necessary resources and support for implementation
- They feel more confident to voice opinions without fear of repercussions
(source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3613)

LMX is grounded in [[social-exchange-theory]]: high-quality exchanges are perceived as incentives from managers, compelling employees to reciprocate with positive behaviors (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3610–3611).

## Measurement

Measured using a 5-item, 7-point Likert scale adapted from Erdogan & Enders (2007) and Zacher et al. (2011). Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.760, Composite Reliability = 0.830, AVE = 0.507 (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3617–3618).
