# Leader-Member Exchange

**Summary**: The quality of the localized, two-way relationship between leaders and their individual subordinates.

**Sources**: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md

---

Leader-Member Exchange (LMX) theory posits that leaders develop different relationships with different employees depending on mutual expectations, trust, and fulfillment over time (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3608).

High-quality LMX interactions are marked by mutual trust, affection, respect, and frequent communication. Within these high-quality exchanges, employees gain more autonomy, receive immediate feedback, secure necessary resources, and have more opportunities to discuss new ideas without fear of harsh repercussions (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3613). 

LMX moderates the impact of [[digital-leadership]] on [[learning-orientation]]. When LMX is strong, the motivating effect of perceived digital leadership on an employee's desire to learn and acquire new skills is significantly amplified (source: Ahmed et al. - 2024.md, p. 3620).
