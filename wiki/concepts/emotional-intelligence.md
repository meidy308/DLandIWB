# Emotional Intelligence

**Summary**: The ability to identify, manage, and regulate one's own emotions and the emotions of others to guide thinking and behavior.

**Sources**: Hadi et al. - 2023.md

---

Emotional intelligence (EI) refers to an individual's capacity to work together effectively, manage stress, lead others, and create innovative work behavior (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 75). It involves psychological and emotional maturity, including tolerance and the ability to control emotions within an organization (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 81).

## Dimensions

Hadi et al. (2024), citing Setiadi & Tampubolon (2020), identify five key elements of emotional intelligence:
1. **Self-awareness**: Identifying feelings and knowing how to calm oneself.
2. **Self-management**: Managing stress and controlling emotions rationally.
3. **Self-motivation**: The ability to recover from failure and improve.
4. **Empathy**: Observing and understanding colleagues' emotions.
5. **Relation-management**: Controlling emotions during conflict and consideration towards others (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 77).

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **As a Dependent Variable (DV)**:
    - **Organizational Support**: Found to have a positive and significant effect on emotional intelligence ($B = 0.587$, $p = 0.000$; source: [[hadi-et-al-2024]]).
    - **Digital Leadership**: Not found to have a significant effect on emotional intelligence ($p = 0.289$; source: [[hadi-et-al-2024]]).

- **As an Independent Variable (IV)**:
    - **Innovative Work Behavior**: Found to have a strong significant positive effect on IWB ($B = 0.587$ total effect, $p = 0.000$; source: [[hadi-et-al-2024]]).

- **As a Mediator**:
    - Mediates the relationship between [[organizational-support]] and [[innovative-work-behavior]] ($B = 0.288$, $p = 0.001$; source: [[hadi-et-al-2024]]).
    - **Does NOT** mediate the relationship between [[digital-leadership]] and [[innovative-work-behavior]] in the Indonesian higher education context ($p = 0.314$; source: [[hadi-et-al-2024]]).

## Measurement

Hadi et al. (2024) used a scale derived from Setiadi & Tampubolon (2020) consisting of 15 statements measured on a questionnaire (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 78). The scale demonstrated high reliability ($\alpha = 0.966$).

## Contradictions

While some theories suggest leadership and emotional intelligence are interrelated (e.g., Pambudy & Handayati, 2022; Ayalew & Ayenew, 2022), [[hadi-et-al-2024]] found no such link between digital leadership and the emotional intelligence of lecturers (source: Hadi et al. - 2023.md, p. 81).
