# Resource-Based Theory

**Summary**: A framework that views an organization’s internal resources and capabilities as the primary drivers of sustainable competitive advantage.

**Sources**: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, Khan et al. - 2025.md

---

Resource-based theory (RBT) posits that for a firm to achieve superior performance, it must possess resources that are valuable, rare, inimitable, and non-substitutable (VRIN). These resources and capabilities determine the firm's sustainable competitive stance (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 401).

## Relationship with Other Variables

### Role of Digital Leadership
In the digital economy, [[digital-leadership]] is categorized as a vital intangible resource. A leader's digital capabilities and knowledge allow an organization to adapt strategies to changing environments and transform digitally, thus enhancing performance (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 401).

### Driving Innovation
RBT explains how internal resources, such as leadership style and employee skills, catalyze [[innovative-work-behavior]] to help firms survive and thrive (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 401).

### Green Innovation and Strategic Agility
Digital leadership is considered a dynamic organizational resource exploitable for securing competitive advantage through green innovation (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 3). Furthermore, [[strategic-agility]] is a dynamic capability that allows companies to optimize these resources, enabling them to pivot swiftly and reconfigure assets toward sustainability (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 5, 12).
