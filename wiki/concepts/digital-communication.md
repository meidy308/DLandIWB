# Digital Communication

**Summary**: The ability to collaborate and interact with others using digital technologies, media, and discussion platforms to achieve work goals.

**Sources**: Zia et al. - 2024.md

---

Digital communication is defined as "the ability to communicate and collaborate with others using digital technologies and media via discussion" (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 3, citing Na-Nan et al., 2019). It encompasses the use of video conferences, social media, and instant messengers for work-related interaction.

## Dimensions

Key aspects of digital communication as a job resource include:
- **Collaboration**: Facilitating teamwork across geographical boundaries.
- **Information Sharing**: Timely exchange of data and insights.
- **Social Support**: Creating a network for emotional and professional assistance in a digital environment.

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Impact on Engagement**: Digital communication is a significant job resource that positively predicts [[digital-engagement]] ($\beta=0.147, p=0.009$) (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 11).
- **Serial Mediation**: It influences [[innovative-work-behavior]] through the sequential mediation of [[digital-engagement]] and [[digital-leadership]] ($\beta=0.074, p=0.009$) (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 12).

## Measurement

- **Na-Nan et al. (2019) Scale**: A 4-item scale assessing interaction quality and tool usage (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 8).
