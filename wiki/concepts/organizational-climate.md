# Organizational Climate

**Summary**: The shared perceptions and collective feelings of employees regarding their work environment, specifically concerning practices, procedures, and behaviors that are rewarded and supported.

**Sources**: [[Soediro et al. - 2025.md]]

---

Organizational climate refers to the "atmosphere" of the workplace as perceived by its members. It encompasses support for creativity, collaboration, and risk-taking, which significantly impacts employees' willingness to engage in innovative activities (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 118, 122). A positive and supportive organizational climate encourages individuals to experiment with new methods and interdisciplinary initiatives (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 349).

## Dimensions

Key aspects of an organizational climate that foster innovation include (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 118, 349):
- **Psychological Safety**: The belief that one will not be punished or humiliated for speaking up with ideas, questions, or mistakes.
- **Support for Creativity**: Practical and emotional encouragement from the organization to generate novel ideas.
- **Collaboration and Inclusiveness**: Environments that promote shared learning and collective problem-solving.
- **Risk-Taking**: The degree to which the organization accepts and encourages experimental approaches that may fail.

## Relationship with Other Variables

Organizational climate is a robust predictor of both cognitive engagement and behavioral outcomes related to innovation (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 349).

- **Predictor of [[innovative-work-behavior]]**: A positive climate significantly enhances IWB (β = 0.251, p = 0.000) (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 257).
- **Predictor of [[creative-process-engagement]]**: Climate encourages involvement in the creative process (β = 0.297, p = 0.000), facilitating collaboration and shared learning (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 259, 363).
- **Indirect effect via [[creative-process-engagement]]**: CPE significantly mediates the relationship between organizational climate and IWB (43.53% mediation) (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 345).

## Measurement

Often measured using surveys looking at perceptions of various climate dimensions. Soediro et al. (2025) used a 5-point Likert scale focusing on supportive climate aspects (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 229).
