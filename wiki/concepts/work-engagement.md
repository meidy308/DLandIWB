# Work Engagement

**Summary**: A positive, fulfilling, work-related state of mind characterized by vigor, dedication, and absorption.

**Sources**: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md

---

Work engagement reflects an individual's energy, dedication, and deep concentration in their work. Engaged individuals possess the motivation to discover new methods to complete their tasks (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3119).

## Dimensions

Work engagement consists of three dimensions (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3114):

1. **Vigor** — High enthusiasm, energy, and mental resilience during work.
2. **Dedication** — Strong commitment, sense of significance, and pride in one's work.
3. **Absorption** — Full involvement and deep concentration in completing tasks.

## Relationship with Other Variables

Work engagement has a positive and significant effect on [[innovative-work-behavior]] (β = 0.262, t = 3.805, p = 0.000). Individuals who are emotionally connected to their work maximize their capabilities, which is reflected in innovative contributions. The effect size is small-to-moderate (F² = 0.082) (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3118–3119).

Theoretically, work engagement is positioned as a critical psychological energy source driven by job resources such as [[digital-leadership]] and [[organization-experience]], consistent with [[job-demands-resources-theory]] and [[conservation-of-resources-theory]] (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3109–3110).

## Measurement

Measured using a 5-point Likert scale with three indicators: vigor, dedication, and absorption. Items adapted from prior literature on work engagement (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3114). Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.816, Composite Reliability = 0.891 (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3117).
