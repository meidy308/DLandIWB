# Work Engagement

**Summary**: A positive, fulfilling, work-related state of mind characterized by vigor, dedication, and absorption.

**Sources**: Bakker and Demerouti - 2007.md, Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, Crawford et al. - 2010.md, Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, Nugroho et al. - 2025.md

---

Work engagement reflects an individual's energy, dedication, and deep concentration. Within the [[job-demands-resources-theory]], work engagement is the core outcome of the [[motivational-process]] driven by [[job-resources]]. These resources fulfill basic psychological needs, leading to the positive affective-motivational state of engagement (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 9).

## Dimensions

Work engagement consists of three dimensions:
1. **Vigor** — High levels of energy and mental resilience.
2. **Dedication** — A sense of significance, enthusiasm, inspiration, pride, and challenge.
3. **Absorption** — Being happily engrossed in work, where time passes quickly (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 14; Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3114).

## Relationship with Other Variables

### Antecedents and Interaction
- **Motivational Process**: Driven primarily by [[job-resources]] (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 9).
- **Challenge vs. Hindrance Demands**: [[challenge-demands]] foster engagement, while [[hindrance-demands]] actively detract from it (source: Crawford et al. - 2010.md, p. 840).
- **[[saliency-hypothesis]]**: Job resources boost engagement most when [[job-demands]] are high. Under pressure, resources become more salient and valuable for maintaining dedication (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 11).

### Reciprocal Relationships (Gain Spirals)
Engaged employees are more likely to mobilize or create their own [[job-resources]] (e.g., seeking help or feedback). This "resource mobilization" creates an upward spiral where engagement builds enduring personal, social, and psychological resources, consistent with **Broaden-and-Build theory** (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 14-15).

### Outcomes
- **Innovative Work Behavior**: Work engagement has a positive and significant effect on [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 88; Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3118–3119).
- **Performance**: Predicts both in-role and extra-role performance (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 11).

## Measurement

- **Utrecht Work Engagement Scale (UWES)**: The most widely used tool (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 23).
- **Afif and Fitrianingrum (2025)**: 5-point Likert scale with vigor, dedication, and absorption indicators (source: Afif and Fitrianingrum - 2025.md, p. 3114).
