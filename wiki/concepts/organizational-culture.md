# Organizational Culture

**Summary**: A system of shared values, beliefs, and hidden assumptions held by members of an organization that guide their behavior and shape the organization's capital and core competencies.

**Sources**: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md

---

Organizational culture is considered organizational capital and a core competency that supports the alignment between organizational values and employees (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 86). It acts as a guide for members' behavior and can be a significant tool for gaining a competitive advantage if aligned with the organization's strategy (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 86–87).

## Dimensions

Based on Robbins and Judge (2018) as cited in Nugroho et al. (2025), organizational culture consists of hidden values and beliefs that proliferate within an organization. In the context of State-Owned Enterprises (SOE) in Indonesia, this is often manifested through core values such as **AKHLAK** (Trustworthy, Competent, Harmonious, Loyal, Adaptive, and Collaborative) (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 85).

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Relationship with [[digital-leadership]]**: Digital leadership has a positive and significant effect on organizational culture (β = 0.311, p = 0.000). A leader's ability to persuade others supports the development of a culture that can face change and achieve sustainable goals (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 87, 92).
- **Relationship with [[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Organizational culture has a positive and significant effect on IWB (β = 0.488, p = 0.000). Culture fosters creativity by pushing workers to embrace innovation as a corporate concept through socialization and the establishment of values such as initiative, risk-taking, and autonomy (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 88, 92).
- **As a Mediator**: Organizational culture significantly mediates the relationship between digital leadership and IWB (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 92, 95).

## Measurement

Measured using instruments developed from Robbins and Judge (2018), consisting of 12 questions (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 90). Reliability in the construction sector context: Cronbach's α = 0.933, Composite Reliability = 0.940 (source: Nugroho et al. - 2025.md, p. 91).
