# Organizational Innovation Climate

**Summary**: The shared perceptions of employees regarding the organization's practices, procedures, and behaviors that encourage and reward innovation.

**Sources**: Huang et al. - 2026.md

---

Organizational innovation climate serves as a contextual asset that reduces perceived risks associated with innovation and emphasizes creative problem-solving. Grounded in [[person-environment-fit-theory]], a strong innovation climate creates an environment congruent with the tools and platforms provided by digital leadership (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 4).

## Dimensions

Adaptations of the concept (e.g., Wang et al., 2017) often include:
- **Work autonomy**: Freedom for employees to set their own work pace and methods.
- **Team collaboration**: Regular exchange of ideas and discussion among colleagues.
- **Organizational incentives**: Reward systems that stimulate innovative enthusiasm (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 5).

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As a Moderator
- **Digital Leadership and IWB**: Organizational innovation climate positively moderates the relationship between [[digital-leadership]] and [[innovative-work-behavior]] ($\beta = .333, p < .001$). The relationship is significantly stronger when the climate is high compared to when it is low (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 9).
- **Risk Reduction**: It reduces the perceived risks of innovation, liberating employees to act on the autonomy granted by digital leaders (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 4).

## Measurement

- **Wang et al. (2017)**: A 10-item scale covering work autonomy, team collaboration, and organizational incentives. Example item: "The company's reward system stimulates employees' innovative enthusiasm" (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 5).
