# Green Organizational Climate

**Summary**: The shared values, perceptions, and assumptions within an organization regarding environmental sustainability that encourage employees to engage in eco-friendly actions.

**Sources**: Khan et al. - 2025.md

---

Green organizational climate emerges from shared environmental values that help push employees toward eco-innovative behaviors (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 5). It is not merely about formal policies but about how employees internalize the environmental mission of the firm as part of their own identity (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 5, citing [[social-identity-theory]]). 

## Dimensions

- **Policy Support**: Clarity and presence of environmental policies.
- **Managerial Support**: Perceptions of leaders' commitment to sustainability.
- **Resource Availability**: Provision of tools and resources for green initiatives.
- **Psychological Safety**: An environment where employees feel safe to experiment with new green solutions.

## Relationship with Other Variables

In the research by [[khan-et-al-2025]], the following relationships were established:
- **Digital Leadership**: DL is a strong enabler of a green climate (β = 0.43, p < .001). Digital platforms facilitate better monitoring and communication of sustainability goals (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).
- **Green Innovative Behavior**: GOC is a solid basis for enhancing green innovative behavior (β = 0.59, p < .001) (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).
- **Strategic Agility**: SA strengthens the impact of digital leadership on the development of a green climate (β = 0.36, p < .001) (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).
- **Mediator Role**: GOC serves as the bridge linking digital leadership to tangible green innovative outcomes (indirect effect = 0.14) (source: Khan et al. - 2025.md, p. 9).

## Measurement

- **Scale**: 4-item scale developed by Norton et al. (2014).
- **Sample items**:
  - "Our company is worried about its environmental impact."
  - "Our company is interested in supporting environmental causes."
  - "Our company believes it is important to protect the environment."
  - "Our company is concerned with becoming more environmentally friendly."
- **Likert**: 5-point scale (1: Strongly Disagree, 5: Strongly Agree).
