# Employee Performance

**Summary**: The work results achieved by an individual in carrying out assigned tasks based on skill, experience, sincerity, and time.

**Sources**: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md

---

Employee performance (Kinerja Karyawan) serves as an evaluation and feedback tool to improve effectiveness, identify weaknesses, and achieve better future results. It can include the achievement of personal goals, such as work-life balance or skill improvement (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 399).

## Dimensions

**Farhaq & Yulianti (2024)** — Views performance through individual capacity to complete tasks efficiently under time pressure (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 397-398).

**Gehan and Yulianti (2024)** — 3 dimensions based on Pradhan & Jena (2017) (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 34):
1. **Task Performance** — Direct job-related behaviors supporting organizational goals.
2. **Contextual Performance** — Socio-psychological behaviors supporting the broader environment.
3. **Adaptive Performance** — Ability to adjust to dynamic work profiles and situations.

**Hang and Nguyệt (2026)** — Defines performance in the retail sector as the level of attendance, ability to meet targets, and timeliness in completing tasks (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 8).

## Relationship with Other Variables

### Predicted by Digital Leadership
- **Significant direct effect**: [[digital-leadership]] significantly predicts employee performance among millennials (β = 2.581, p = 0.010) (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 405). Similarly, in the retail sector, digital leadership is positively related to salesperson performance ($\beta = 0.203, p = .000$) (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 13).
- **Non-significant direct effect**: In a similar millennial study, digital leadership did not directly predict performance ($p = 0.464$), suggesting the connection varies even within similar demographics (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 36).

### Predicted by Innovative Work Behavior
[[innovative-work-behavior]] consistently has a significant positive effect on employee performance (β = 5.315, p = 0.000 in Farhaq; $O = 0.541, p = .000$ in Gehan; $\beta = 0.349, p = .000$ in Hang & Nguyệt). The activities of building, promoting, and realizing ideas improve an employee's overall capacity to perform (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 406; Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 37; Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 13).

### Mediated Relationship
[[innovative-work-behavior]] acts as a significant mediator between [[digital-leadership]] and employee performance (β = 5.023, p = 0.000). For millennials, digital leadership's value is realized through the innovation it stimulates, which then drives performance (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 407; Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 37).
- **Sequential Mediation**: In retail contexts, performance is driven by a chain starting from **[[digital-leadership]]** to **[[digital-literacy]]**, then **[[innovative-work-behavior]]**, and finally to work performance ($\beta = 0.127, p = .000$) (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 16).

### Moderated Relationship
- **[[digital-literacy]] moderates DL → Performance**: The impact of digital leadership on performance is significantly strengthened when employees have high digital literacy ($\beta = 0.081, p = .010$) (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 16).

## Measurement

- **Farhaq & Yulianti (2024)**: 18-item scale. Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.965, CR = 0.962, AVE = 0.622 (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 403-404).
- **Gehan and Yulianti (2024)**: 8 indicators (after validation), 5-point Likert. Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.887, CR = 0.910, AVE = 0.561 (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 35-36).
- **Hang and Nguyệt (2026)**: 4 items (timeliness, minimal effort, taking challenges, up-to-date knowledge), 5-point Likert (Koopmans et al., 2012). Reliability: $\alpha = 0.887$, AVE = 0.748 (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 10).

## Contradictions

The direct significant influence of digital leadership on performance found in **Farhaq & Yulianti (2024)** and **Hang and Nguyệt (2026)** contradicts **Gehan and Yulianti (2024)** and **Arief Rizqi et al. (2023)**, who found that digital leadership does not always significantly predict performance directly in all contexts (source: Gehan and Yulianti - 2024.md, p. 37; farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 400).
- **Non-significant moderation**: While digital literacy moderates leading paths, it does not significantly moderate the linkage between IWB and Performance in the retail sector (source: Hang and Nguyệt - 2026.md, p. 16).
