# Employee Performance

**Summary**: The work results achieved by an individual in carrying out assigned tasks based on skill, experience, sincerity, and time.

**Sources**: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md

---

Employee performance (Kinerja Karyawan) serves as an evaluation and feedback tool to improve effectiveness, identify weaknesses, and achieve better future results. It can include the achievement of personal goals, such as work-life balance or skill improvement (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 399).

## Dimensions

In the study of millennials in East Java, employee performance is viewed through the lens of individual capacity to complete tasks efficiently under time pressure and high-stress environments (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 397-398).

## Relationship with Other Variables

### Predicted by Digital Leadership
[[digital-leadership]] significantly predicts employee performance (β = 2.581, p = 0.010). Leaders with digital knowledge and experience drive subordinates' performance by utilizing digital resources effectively (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 405).

### Predicted by Innovative Work Behavior
[[innovative-work-behavior]] has a significant positive effect on employee performance (β = 5.315, p = 0.000). The activities of problem recognition, idea generation, promotion, and realization improve an employee's overall capacity to innovate and perform (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 406).

### Mediated Relationship
[[innovative-work-behavior]] acts as a significant mediator between [[digital-leadership]] and employee performance (β = 5.023, p = 0.000) (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 407). 

## Measurement

**Farhaq & Yulianti (2024)**: Measured using an 18-item scale. Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.965, CR = 0.962, AVE = 0.622 (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 403-404).

## Contradictions

The direct significant influence of digital leadership on performance found in recent millennial-focused studies contradicts **Arief Rizqi et al. (2023)**, who found that digital leadership does not always significantly predict performance directly in all contexts (source: farhaq-and-yulianti-2024.md, p. 400).
