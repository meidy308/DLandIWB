# Person x Situation Approach of JD-R

**Summary**: An approach that combines stable individual personality traits with daily fluctuations in job demands and resources to explain momentary and chronic well-being.

**Sources**: Bakker et al. - 2023.md

---

The Person × Situation approach (Bakker, 2015) acknowledges that while job characteristics fluctuate daily, individuals have stable personality traits that moderate how they perceive and react to these fluctuations (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 37).

## Dimensions

- **Stable Person Factors**: Personality traits (e.g., Big Five) and chronic well-being levels (e.g., chronic [[burnout]]).
- **Dynamic Situation Factors**: Daily fluctuations in [[job-demands]], [[job-resources]], and [[personal-resources]].
- **Daily Well-being**: Fluctuations in daily [[work-engagement]], [[exhaustion]], and behaviors ([[job-crafting]], [[self-undermining]]) (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 37, Figure 2).

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Moderation by Personality**: 
    - Neurotic individuals suffer more on days with high workload (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 37).
    - Extraverted individuals flourish more on days with high social resources (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 38).
- **Moderation by Chronic States**: 
    - Chronic ill-being (e.g., burnout) amplifies the impact of daily job demands on daily exhaustion (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 38).
    - High general well-being (e.g., vitality) amplifies the impact of daily job resources on daily work engagement and job crafting (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 38).
- **Loss and Gain Cycles**: Daily cycles of self-undermining (loss) and job crafting (gain) are nested within these person-situation interactions (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 37).

## Measurement

Typically requires daily diary or experience sampling designs (within-person) combined with baseline personality assessments (between-person). Analyzed using multilevel modeling (Level 1: days; Level 2: individuals) (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 37).
