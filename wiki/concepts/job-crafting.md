# Job Crafting

**Summary**: The self-initiated actions employees take to reshape their job boundaries by aligning their knowledge, skills, and psychological resources with work demands.

**Sources**: Huang et al. - 2026.md, Wang et al. - 2025.md

---

Job crafting is a proactive behavior where employees autonomously adjust their work tasks, methods, and interpersonal relationships to better suit their personal needs and goals. Grounded in [[self-determination-theory]], it embodies employee autonomy and serves as a crucial catalyst for innovation (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 3).

## Dimensions

Standard literature identifies two main dimensional frameworks:

### JD-R Based Framework (Tims et al., 2013)
- **Increasing structural job resources**: Seeking opportunities for professional development and learning.
- **Increasing social job resources**: Seeking feedback and support from colleagues and supervisors.
- **Increasing challenging job demands**: Seeking more complex tasks or taking on new responsibilities.
- **Decreasing hindering job demands**: Reducing aspects of the job that interfere with performance (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 5, 10).

### Role-Based Framework (Wrzesniewski and Dutton, 2001)
- **Task Crafting**: Changing the number, scope, or type of tasks performed at work.
- **Cognitive Crafting**: Changing how one perceives the job and its meaning/purpose.
- **Relational Crafting**: Changing the quality and quantity of interpersonal interactions at work (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 2, 6).

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As a Mediator
- **Digital Leadership and IWB**: Job crafting mediates the relationship between [[digital-leadership]] and [[innovative-work-behavior]]. Specifically:
    - **General mediation**: Point estimate = 0.075, 95% CI [0.014, 0.152] (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 8).
    - **Task Crafting**: Significant mediator (B = 0.082, p < .001) (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).
    - **Cognitive Crafting**: Significant mediator (B = 0.148, p < .001) (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 8).
    - **Relational Crafting**: Found to be a **non-significant** mediator in the manufacturing sector (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 8, 13).
- **Sequential Mediation Path**: Job crafting initiates a sequential mediation chain: [[digital-leadership]] $\rightarrow$ [[job-crafting]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovation-efficacy]] $\rightarrow$ [[innovative-work-behavior]] (Point estimate = 0.067, 95% CI [0.033, 0.124]) (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 8).

### As a Consequent
- **Digital Leadership**: Digital leadership promotes job crafting by providing advanced digital tools, infrastructure, and flexible work models that increase autonomy and "Can Do" motivation (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 3; Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 3).

### As an Antecedent
- **Innovative Performance**: All three forms of crafting (Task, Cognitive, Relational) are hypothesized to drive innovation, though Relational Crafting effects may be constrained by organizational structure (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 10, 13).

## Measurement

- **Tims et al. (2013)**: A 15-item scale selected from the original 21-item scale. Items include "I make efforts to enhance my skills" and "I occasionally determine my own approach to work" (source: Huang et al. - 2026.md, p. 5).
- **Slemp and Vella-Brodrick (2013)**: A 15-item scale measuring Task, Cognitive, and Relational crafting. Items include "Introduce new approaches to improve your work" and "Think about how your job gives your life purpose" (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 6).
