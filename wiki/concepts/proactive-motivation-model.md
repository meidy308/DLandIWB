# Proactive Motivation Model

**Summary**: A framework explaining how individuals cultivate proactive behaviors through three motivational states: "Can Do," "Reason To," and "Energized To."

**Sources**: Wang et al. - 2025.md

---

Introduced by **Parker et al. (2010)**, the proactive motivation model describes proactivity as a motivated, deliberate, and goal-directed process. It explains how external factors, such as leadership, influence employee actions by shaping their motivational states (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 2).

## Dimensions

The model is built on three pillars (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 3):
- **"Can Do" Motivation**: Reflects self-efficacy and the assessment of task feasibility. It is strengthened when leaders provide technological support and training.
- **"Reason To" Motivation**: Relates to intrinsic and integrated motivation. It is stimulated when employees find meaning, autonomy, and alignment with their values.
- **"Energized To" Motivation**: Underscores the role of positive emotional states, such as enthusiasm and excitement, in fueling proactive efforts.

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As an Explanatory Framework
- **Digital Leadership and Job Crafting**: [[digital-leadership]] influences [[job-crafting]] by reinforcing these three states. Digital leaders strengthen "Can Do" via tech support, "Reason To" via autonomy, and "Energized To" via an innovative environment (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 3).
- **Proactive Behavior**: The model serves as the foundational theory for explaining how job crafting (a proactive behavior) leads to [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Wang et al. - 2025.md, p. 2).
