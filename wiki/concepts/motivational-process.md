# Motivational Process

**Summary**: One of the two dual pathways in [[job-demands-resources-theory]], describing how job resources lead to engagement and positive organizational outcomes.

**Sources**: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, Bakker et al. - 2003.md

---

The Motivational Process is a pathway within the JD-R framework driven by the availability of [[job-resources]] (e.g., social support, autonomy, feedback). These resources are functional in achieving work goals and fulfill basic psychological needs like autonomy, competence, and relatedness (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 9).

## Dimensions

- **Need Satisfaction**: Resources fulfill intrinsic human needs.
- **Engagement**: Manifests as [[work-engagement]] (vigor, dedication, absorption).
- **Positive Outcomes**: Leads to organizational commitment, high performance, and reduced turnover intentions (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 10).

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Predictor**: [[job-resources]] (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 9).
- **Mediator**: [[work-engagement]] mediates the link between resources and performance/commitment (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 11).
- **Outcomes**: Increases [[job-performance]] and [[affective-commitment]] (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 11).

## Measurement

Typically measured using SEM paths from resources to engagement and then to commitment or performance metrics.

## Contradictions

The process is amplified by challenging demands (see [[saliency-hypothesis]]), suggesting that resources alone are not the only driver of motivation (source: Bakker and Demerouti - 2014.md, p. 11).
