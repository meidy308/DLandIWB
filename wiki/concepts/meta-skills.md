# Meta-skills

**Summary**: Higher-order capabilities such as cooperation, creativity, and problem-solving that enable individuals to adapt to changing technological contexts and multi-track employment biographies.

**Sources**: Demerouti - 2022.md

---

Meta-skills are broad, adaptable competencies that go beyond specific technical knowledge. In the context of digitalization and automation, they are essential for maintaining a "human and technology symbiosis" where work remains meaningful and leads to self-actualization (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1206, 1208).

## Dimensions

According to Demerouti (2022, p. 1208), key meta-skills include:
- **Capability for Cooperation**: Working effectively with others (and potentially AI/robots) in decentralized production systems.
- **Creativity**: Generating novel and useful ideas to leverage technological potential.
- **Problem-solving**: Navigating the complexities of less transparent technical solutions and new routines.

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **[[personal-resources]]**: Meta-skills act as personal resources that help employees manage the new demands posed by technology.
- **[[adaptive-performance]]**: High meta-skills are likely predictors of adaptive performance as they enable employees to integrate change into their daily work (source: Demerouti - 2022.md, p. 1208).
- **[[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Creativity and problem-solving are core components of IWB.
- **[[digital-leadership]]**: Developing these skills in followers is a key responsibility of leaders in the digital age.

## Measurement

Demerouti (2022) does not provide a specific scale, but suggests that educational institutions should prioritize these over purely knowledge-based teaching.
