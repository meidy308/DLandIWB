# Playful Work Design

**Summary**: A proactive strategy where employees create conditions at work that foster play and enjoyment without changing the actual design of the job.

**Sources**: Bakker et al. - 2023.md

---

Playful Work Design (PWD) involves employees intentionally making their work more enjoyable or challenging through fun and competition (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40). It is a form of cognitive and behavioral redesign that optimizes the person-task fit by increasing resources and challenges in a playful manner.

## Dimensions

Bakker et al. (2023, p. 40) identify two main dimensions:
- **Designing Fun**: Using humor and fantasy to make work more enjoyable (e.g., imagining a task is a scene from a movie).
- **Designing Competition**: Intentionally making tasks more challenging to increase engagement (e.g., guessing customer behavior or timing oneself against a personal best).

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Satisfaction of Basic Needs**: Designing fun satisfies the need for **relatedness**, while designing competition satisfies the need for **competence**. Both satisfy the need for **autonomy** (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40-41).
- **Buffer against Hindrance Demands**: On days when employees use PWD, hindrance demands (like repetitive tasks or interpersonal conflicts) do not result in reduced [[work-engagement]] or impaired [[job-performance]] (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 41).
- **Positive correlation with [[job-performance]]**: PWD is linked to better performance during both busy and quiet days (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 41).

## Measurement

Measured using scales developed by Scharp et al. (2022) focusing on the frequency and intensity of fun and competition design at work (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40).
