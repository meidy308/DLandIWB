# School Culture

**Summary**: School culture refers to the deep-seated values, beliefs, and shared agreements that shape the interactions and learning orientation within an educational community.

**Sources**: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md

---

School culture is a set of shared agreements that form a unified community for effective learning orientation. It reflects the fundamental reasons why members of a school act in certain ways, rooted in deep-seated values and beliefs (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 33). It is often distinguished from school climate; while climate reflects actions based on values that can change quickly, culture refers to the underlying foundations and changes more gradually (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 33).

## Dimensions

Key elements and dimensions of school culture in the context of innovative behavior include:
- **Collaboration and Collegiality**: Interaction and cooperation among teachers (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 33).
- **Professional Values**: Emphasis on learning and professional growth (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 33).
- **Collaborative Planning**: Shared involvement in planning educational activities (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 33).
- **Transformational Leadership**: The role of leadership in shaping and maintaining the culture (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 33).

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As an Independent Variable (IV)
- **Positive effect on Innovative Work Behavior (IWB)**: School culture significantly predicts teacher IWB. A culture that provides opportunities for collaboration, feedback, and professional development encourages teachers to explore and implement new ideas (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 40).
- **Correlation with Job Satisfaction**: School culture, along with self-efficacy, is significantly correlated with teacher job satisfaction (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 33).
- **Influence on Technology Integration**: School culture plays a role in determining how technology is used as a tool for delivering and enriching learning materials (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 32).

## Measurement

In the study by Tanan & Purbojo (2025), school culture was measured using indicators such as collaboration, professional development, and leadership support, using a 4-point Likert scale (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 35).

## Contradictions

None noted specifically for school culture, though its distinction from school climate is highlighted as a point of conceptual clarification (source: Tanan and Purbojo - 2025.md, p. 33).
