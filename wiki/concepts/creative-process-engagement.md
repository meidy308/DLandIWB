# Creative Process Engagement

**Summary**: The active involvement of individuals in cognitive and collaborative activities associated with the early stages of creativity, including problem identification, information searching, and idea generation.

**Sources**: [[Soediro et al. - 2025.md]]

---

Creative process engagement (CPE) describes the extent to which an individual immerses themselves in the creative phases of their work. In the context of higher education, it refers to how lecturers actively engage in ideation, problem-solving, and experimentation to enhance teaching and research (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 139). It is considered the "missing link" that transforms individual or organizational intentions into actual practical innovation (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 140).

## Dimensions

Key dimensions of creative process engagement include (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 140):
- **Problem Identification**: Recognizing and defining the core issues that require creative solutions.
- **Information Searching**: Seeking out relevant data, pedagogical tools, or research methodologies to address identified problems.
- **Idea Generation**: Producing novel and useful concepts or strategies to improve work outcomes.

## Relationship with Other Variables

Creative process engagement often acts as a critical mediator between individual/organizational antecedents and [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 143).

- **Mediator of [[digital-leadership]]**: CPE accounts for 47.94% of the effect of digital leadership on innovative work behavior (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 143).
- **Mediator of [[organizational-climate]]**: CPE accounts for 43.53% of the effect of organizational climate on innovative work behavior (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 143).
- **Mediator of [[growth-mindset]]**: CPE accounts for 31.84% of the effect of growth mindset on innovative work behavior (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 143).
- **Predictor of [[innovative-work-behavior]]**: Higher involvement in the creative process directly leads to higher levels of innovative work behavior (β = 0.321, p < 0.05) (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 147).

## Measurement

CPE is typically measured using Likert scales (1-5) adapted from established creativity research. Soediro et al. (2025) utilized indicators focusing on active participation in cognitive and collaborative creative activities (source: Soediro et al. - 2025.md, p. 142).
