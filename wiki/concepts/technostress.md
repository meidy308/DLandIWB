# Technostress

**Summary**: The negative psychological impact and strain experienced by individuals due to their use of, or dependence on, digital technology in the workplace.

**Sources**: Scholze and Hecker - 2024.md

---

Technostress represents the "dark side" of digitization in the workplace. It is the psychological strain and anxiety that occurs when individuals feel pressured by the fast-paced, ever-evolving nature of digital work and feel compelled to keep up with technological advancements. In the context of the [[job-demands-resources-theory]], it is often viewed as an outcome of high [[digital-job-demands]] (source: Scholze and Hecker - 2024.md, p. 2).

## Dimensions and Creators

Technostress is driven by several "creators" or [[digital-job-demands]], including:
- **Technology Dependence**: The necessity to use technology to complete basic work tasks, leading to stress when systems fail.
- **Communication Overload**: Constant interruptions and the pressure to be always available via digital channels.
- **Work Intensification**: The perception that technology forces employees to work faster or handle a larger volume of work.

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Strain (Synonym/Component)**: Technostress is highly related to, and often measured as a specific form of, [[strain]] (e.g., exhaustion, burnout) caused specifically by Information and Communication Technologies (DICT) (source: Scholze and Hecker - 2024.md, p. 2).
- **Well-being**: Technostress negatively impacts psychological and physical well-being.
- **Digital Job Demands (Antecedent)**: High levels of [[digital-job-demands]] such as work intensification and availability directly increase technostress (source: Scholze and Hecker - 2024.md, p. 8).

## Mitigation

To reduce the detrimental impact of technostress, organizations can employ interventions such as providing technical support, promoting digital health protection (e.g., "digital detox"), and ensuring that [[digital-job-resources]] (like autonomy and efficiency) are maximized to buffer the negative effects (source: Scholze and Hecker - 2024.md, p. 9).

## Contradictions

Recent perspectives suggest that technostress might not solely be negative. Depending on individual appraisal and organizational context, some aspects of technology-induced pressure might act as [[challenge-demands]] rather than purely [[hindrance-demands]], suggesting a more nuanced view of its role within organizations (source: Scholze and Hecker - 2024.md, p. 8).
