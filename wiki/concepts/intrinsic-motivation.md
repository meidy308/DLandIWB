# Intrinsic Motivation

**Summary**: An internal drive to act out of interest, satisfaction, or a desire to achieve personal goals, rather than for external rewards.

**Sources**: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md

---

Intrinsic motivation originates from inside an individual and is critical for driving innovation. When employees feel intrinsically motivated, they become more committed, proactive, and resilient in the face of challenges (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 45). They explore new ideas without relying purely on external compensation, such as bonuses or promotions.

Studies show that [[digital-leadership]] has a strong positive effect on increasing an employee's intrinsic motivation (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 51). Digital leaders contribute to this by offering autonomy through flexible work arrangements and using digital analytics to provide real-time feedback and recognition, helping employees realize the impact of their contributions.

Consequently, intrinsic motivation is a significant driver of [[innovative-work-behavior]]. Furthermore, based on [[social-role-theory]], research indicates that the impact of digital leadership on intrinsic motivation is stronger for women than for men (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 52).
