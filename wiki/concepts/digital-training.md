# Digital Training

**Summary**: Organizational efforts to improve employees' digital skills, knowledge, and competencies to achieve business objectives.

**Sources**: Zia et al. - 2024.md

---

Digital training refers to the systematic initiatives by organizations to enhance the "digital skills, knowledge, competencies, and capabilities" of their workforce (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 3, citing Chikazhe & Nyakunuwa, 2022). It is often conceptualized as a job resource within the [[job-demands-resources-theory]].

## Dimensions

- **Skill Development**: Enhancing practical technical abilities.
- **System Awareness**: Broadening knowledge of the organization's technological infrastructure.
- **Contextual Application**: Training tailored to specific work roles and needs.

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Predictor of Engagement**: While theoretically expected to boost engagement, research by Zia et al. (2024) found a **non-significant** relationship with [[digital-engagement]] among technological professionals ($\beta=0.059, p=0.141$).
- **Indirect Effects**: The indirect impact on [[innovative-work-behavior]] through mediation was also non-significant in the same study ($\beta=0.024, p=0.296$) (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 12).

## Measurement

- **Adapted Training Scale**: Zia et al. (2024) adapted a 5-item scale from Schmidt (2004), focusing on perceived availability and quality of digital training.

## Contradictions

- **Routine vs. Value**: While generic training is often seen as a resource, technological professionals may perceive it as a repetitive "obligation" or a "formal requirement" rather than a meaningful investment, leading to a lack of impact on engagement (source: Zia et al. - 2024.md, p. 13).
