# Proactive Vitality Management

**Summary**: A form of proactive behavior where employees intentionally manage their physical and psychological resources to ensure optimal functioning at work.

**Sources**: Bakker et al. - 2023.md

---

Proactive Vitality Management (PVM) is a self-oriented proactive behavior where individuals take initiative to improve their own energy and well-being (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40). Unlike [[job-crafting]], which focuses on changing the work environment (situation), PVM focuses on changing the self. Examples include networking for inspiration, taking intentional breaks in nature, or seeking out energizing interactions (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40).

## Dimensions

PVM is typically measured as a unidimensional construct of self-initiated energy management, though it involves:
- **Physical energy management**: Actions to maintain physical stamina.
- **Psychological resource management**: Actions to maintain motivation and inspiration (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40).

## Relationship with Other Variables

- **Predictor of [[work-engagement]]**: PVM has a positive relationship with weekly work engagement (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40).
- **Predictor of [[employee-creativity]]**: Weekly PVM is positively related to changes in weekly creativity through work engagement, especially for those with a high [[learning-orientation]] (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40).
- **Mitigator of [[burnout]]**: Chinese employees using PVM reported fewer complaints of emotional exhaustion (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40).

## Measurement

Measured using the Proactive Vitality Management Scale (Op den Kamp et al., 2018), which includes items about intentional actions to maintain energy and inspiration (source: Bakker et al. - 2023.md, p. 40).
