# Digital Talent Development

**Summary**: The systematic process of enhancing employees' digital skills, technical proficiency, and adaptability through training, upskilling, and reskilling initiatives.

**Sources**: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md

---

Digital talent development (DTD) goes beyond simple technical skill enhancement; it focuses on fostering a supportive digital culture, developing digital leadership, and building collaborative competencies that drive innovation (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 476). In the context of the digital economy, systematic talent development is a strategic imperative to address technological change and skill disruption.

## Dimensions

Standard indicators for DTD include:
- Technical skill enhancement
- Upskilling and reskilling programs
- Career planning for digital roles
- Professional growth initiatives (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483)

## Relationship with Other Variables

### As an Independent Variable (IV)
- **[[innovative-work-behavior]]**: DTD provides the foundation for innovation but often lacks a significant direct effect. In Fauziah et al. (2026), the direct path was non-significant ($B = 0.076, p = 0.202$), suggesting that DTD requires mediators to influence behavior.

### Predictor of Mediators
DTD positively and significantly influences the organizational enablers that mediate its impact on innovation:
- **[[digital-leadership]]**: Helps identify and nurture leaders equipped with digital competencies (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 482).
- **[[digital-competency]]**: Enhances technical proficiency and problem-solving capabilities (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483).
- **[[digital-organizational-culture]]**: Helps talents strengthen their confidence in utilizing technology, which fosters an environment for sharing ideas (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 483).

## Measurement

In the study by Fauziah et al. (2026), DTD was measured using 5 indicators. Reliability testing showed a Cronbach's Alpha of 0.915 and an AVE of 0.628 (source: Fauziah et al. - 2026.md, p. 480).
