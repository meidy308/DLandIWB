# Employee Creativity

**Summary**: The ability of employees to generate novel and useful ideas regarding products, services, processes, and methods within an organization.

**Sources**: Yulianti and Maharani - 2024 .md, Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, Umayna et al. - 2025.md, Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md

---

Employee creativity (Kreativitas Karyawan) is the production of new and potentially useful ideas by an individual or a small group of individuals working together. It is often seen as the first stage of innovation, where ideas are generated before being implemented through [[innovative-work-behavior]] (source: Yulianti and Maharani - 2024 .md, p. 161).

## Dimensions

Creativity is often measured through two primary lenses (source: Yulianti and Maharani - 2024 .md, p. 149):
1. **Novelty** — The ability to be a pioneer in generating new ideas or methods.
2. **Utility** — The usefulness or potential implementation of the new idea or solution for the company.

## Relationship with Other Variables

### Predicted by Digital Leadership
Digital leadership significantly and positively predicts employee creativity. Leaders who articulate a clear digital vision and possess technical competence stimulate creative thinking among subordinates by providing the necessary resources and environment for experimentation (source: Yulianti and Maharani - 2024 .md, p. 158; Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 50).

### Relationship with Performance
There is conflicting evidence regarding the direct link between creativity and performance:
- **Non-significant direct effect**: In some contexts, such as at PT Semen Indonesia, higher creativity does not directly translate into improved [[employee-performance]] ($p = 0.096$). This is attributed to the fact that creativity is a "potential" state that requires implementation (through IWB) to impact performance (source: Yulianti and Maharani - 2024 .md, p. 161).
- **Negative Moderation**: Among Gen Z/Y employees, high creativity can actually weaken the positive impact of digital leadership on performance, as creative individuals may prefer independent action over leadership direction (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 251).

### Relationship with Innovative Work Behavior
Creativity is the "raw material" for [[innovative-work-behavior]]. While they are related, they are distinct constructs; creativity focuses on idea generation, while IWB includes the promotion and realization/implementation of those ideas (source: Yulianti and Maharani - 2024 .md, p. 161).

## Measurement

- **Yulianti and Maharani (2024)**: 4 items (novelty and utility) based on Farmer (2003). Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.889, CR = 0.872, AVE = 0.724 (source: Yulianti and Maharani - 2024 .md, p. 153).
- **Hastiti et al. (2025)**: 6 indicators focusing on new ideas and innovative approaches (source: Hastiti et al. - 2025.md, p. 47).
- **Rahmandika et al. (2025)**: 4 items (adapted from Baer & Oldham, 2006). Reliability: Cronbach's α = 0.753, CR = 0.848, AVE = 0.669 (source: Rahmandika et al. - 2025.md, p. 248).
